House Commemoration 8023
Introduced by: Representative Pourier
1 A LEGISLATIVE COMMEMORATION honoring the legacy and strength of
2 grandmothers in this state.
3 WHEREAS, grandmothers form the quiet backbone of communities, providing an example
4 of love, wisdom, and resilience for generations; and
5 WHEREAS, grandmothers are the bedrock of families, often stepping in as caregivers,
6 providers, and nurturers when circumstances demand, raising grandchildren with the same
7 devotion they gave their own children; and
8 WHEREAS, in Lakota, Dakota, and Nakota traditions, grandmothers, known as unci,
9 embody sacred authority as wisdom keepers, ceremonial leaders, and decision-makers whose
10 guidance ensures cultural continuity, language preservation, and the passing of ancestral
11 teachings to the sacred children; and
12 WHEREAS, grandmothers of all backgrounds in this state sacrifice daily to feed, clothe,
13 educate, and protect their families, volunteering in schools, churches, and tribal programs
14 while modeling hard work, thrift, forgiveness, and the grit required to thrive on the farm,
15 ranch, reservation, or city block; and
16 WHEREAS, grandmothers teach by example the values of hospitality, humility, prayer,
17 and perseverance, standing firm against hardship, addiction, loss, or division, while lifting
18 others with encouragement and steadfast faith; and
19 WHEREAS, the legacy of grandmothers endures in the laughter of grandchildren, the
20 strength of great-grandchildren, and the moral compass of communities they shaped through
21 decades of faithful service, often without thanks or acclaim;
