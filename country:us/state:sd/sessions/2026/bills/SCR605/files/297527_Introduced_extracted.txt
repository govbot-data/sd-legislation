26.816.16 101st Legislative Session SCR605
2026 South Dakota Legislature
Senate Concurrent Resolution 605
Introduced by: Senator Wipf
1 A CONCURRENT RESOLUTION recognizing the significance of the monarch butterfly
2 and encouraging cooperative, voluntary conservation efforts in South Dakota.
3 WHEREAS, in 2025, the United States Fish and Wildlife Service proposed listing the
4 monarch butterfly, Danaus plexippus, as a threatened species under the Endangered Species
5 Act; and
6 WHEREAS, the monarch butterfly is a widely recognized and iconic species that contributes
7 to pollination and the overall health of natural ecosystems; and
8 WHEREAS, monarch butterflies depend upon milkweed plants, which are the only plants
9 on which monarch butterflies lay their eggs and upon which monarch larvae feed; and
10 WHEREAS, habitat loss, including the decline of milkweed, has contributed to reductions
11 in monarch butterfly populations; and
12 WHEREAS, lands used for agriculture, transportation rights-of-way, and state
13 management can, when appropriate, provide habitat beneficial to monarch butterflies while
14 continuing to serve their primary purposes; and
15 WHEREAS, conservation efforts are most effective when they are voluntary, cooperative,
16 and respectful of private property rights and agricultural operations;
17 NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED, by the Senate of the One Hundred First Legislature
18 of the State of South Dakota, the House of Representatives concurring therein, that the
19 Legislature recognizes the importance of the monarch butterfly and supports voluntary
20 conservation practices that are compatible with agriculture, roadside management, and state
21 land management; and
22 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that the Legislature encourages state agencies, local
23 governments, and other land managers to preserve existing milkweed where practicable, and
