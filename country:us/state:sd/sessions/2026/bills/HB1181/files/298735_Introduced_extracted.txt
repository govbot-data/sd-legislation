26.192.23 101st Legislative Session 1181
2026 South Dakota Legislature
House Bill 1181
Introduced by: Representative Andera
1 An Act to prohibit weather and climate modification activities and provide a penalty
2 therefor.
3 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF SOUTH DAKOTA:
4 Section 1. That a NEW SECTION be added to chapter 34A-1:
5 There is created in the state treasury the weather and climate modification
6 monitoring fund. This fund consists of fines received pursuant to section 2 of this Act and
7 all other moneys designated for deposit in the fund.
8 The Department of Agriculture and Natural Resources shall administer the fund.
9 The purpose of the fund is to defray expenses associated with monitoring for prohibited
10 weather modification activities, investigating complaints, assessing civil penalties, and
11 monitoring for compliance. The department may use one-half of the moneys received
12 pursuant to section 2 of this Act to defray the expenses of activities associated with
13 administering sections 1 to 4, inclusive, of this Act. The department shall provide one-half
14 of the moneys received pursuant to section 2 of this Act to the Department of
15 Transportation to defray any expenses associated with administering sections 5 to 8,
16 inclusive, of this Act.
17 Interest on moneys credited to the fund must remain in the fund.
18 Expenditures from the fund must be budgeted through the general appropriation
19 bill.
20 Section 2. That a NEW SECTION be added to chapter 34A-1:
21 No person may inject, release, or disperse, by any means, any amount of a
22 chemical, a chemical compound, or a substance, which is known to cause negative health
23 impacts in toxic amounts, into the atmosphere, for the express purpose of modifying
24 atmosphere, intensity of sunlight, temperature, or weather.

10 Section 3. That a NEW SECTION be added to chapter 34A-1:
11 The department shall establish an online form for persons to use in reporting
12 suspected violations of section 2 of this Act. The form must allow persons to report the
13 following, if known:
14 (1) A business name;
15 (2) The address of the alleged perpetrator;
16 (3) Operating hours of the company;
17 (4) The name of the majority owner of the company; and
18 (5) A list of chemicals, chemical compounds, or substances dispersed by the company
19 into the atmosphere.

25 Act received:
26 (a) From individuals; or
27 (b) From airports that are required to file quarterly reports pursuant to section
