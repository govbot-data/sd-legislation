AN ACT
ENTITLED An Act to create a taskforce to study the creation of Indian medicaid
managed care entities in the state.
BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF SOUTH DAKOTA:
Section 1. There is established the Indian Medicaid Managed Care Model taskforce to study
the creation of Indian medicaid managed care entities, as defined in 42 U.S.C. § 1396u-2(h),
in this state. The taskforce must consist of:
(1) A representative from each federally recognized Indian tribe located wholly or
partially within the state, appointed by the appropriate tribal council;
(2) The secretary of the Department of Social Services, or the secretary's designee;
(3) The secretary of the Department of Health, or the secretary's designee;
(4) The secretary of the Department of Tribal Relations, or the secretary's designee;
(5) Two members of the House of Representatives, appointed by the speaker of the
House of Representatives;
(6) Two members of the Senate, appointed by the president pro tempore of the
Senate;
(7) A representative of the United States Bureau of Indian Affairs, appointed by the
director of the bureau; and
(8) The following members, appointed by the Executive Board of the Legislative
Research Council:
(a) One member representing a healthcare provider;
(b) One member representing a healthcare insurance provider; and
(c) One healthcare administrator.
Any vacancy on the taskforce must be filled in the same manner as the original
appointment.
At the initial meeting, the members of the taskforce shall select one from among
themselves to serve as the chair and one to serve as the vice chair.

The Department of Social Services shall provide administrative support to the
taskforce.
The taskforce shall seek input from state and federal executive branch agencies,
representatives of the Indian tribes, representatives of the health care industry, and other
relevant stakeholders.
The taskforce shall report its findings and recommendations, together with any
proposed legislation, to the Governor, on or before December 1, 2028.
