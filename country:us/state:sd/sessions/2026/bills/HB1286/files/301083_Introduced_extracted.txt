26.882.29 101st Legislative Session 1286
2026 South Dakota Legislature
House Bill 1286
Introduced by: Representative Overweg
1 An Act to amend the process by which moneys are distributed from the employer's
2 investment in South Dakota's future fund.
3 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF SOUTH DAKOTA:
4 Section 1. That § 61-5-29.1 be AMENDED:
5 61-5-29.1. There is hereby created in the state treasury a special revenue fund
6 to be known as the employer's investment in South Dakota's future fund. Moneys in the
7 fund must be used for purposes related to, to be administered by the Governor's Office of
8 Economic Development, for the purpose of making grants or awards for research and or
9 economic development for the projects in this state.
10 For purposes of this section, section 61-5-29.3, and sections 3 to 6, inclusive, of
11 this Act, "a research or economic development project" means:
12 (1) Research or equipment required in support of key industries, universities, or
13 industries determined to be viable through an economic analysis of this state, a
14 specific community, or the research project;
15 (2) Scholarships to postsecondary educational institutions operated by the Board of
16 Regents or the South Dakota Board of Technical Education, provided that the
17 scholarships are for programs that prepare a student for a career field available in
18 this state;
19 (3) Workforce development, apprentice programs, workforce trainings, workforce
20 recruitment, or career and technical education programs offered through a school
21 district, accredited nonpublic school, or institution subject to oversight by the Board
22 of Regents or the South Dakota Board of Technical Education;
23 (4) The development of infrastructure required to support economic development in
24 this state;
25 (5) Projects that support business retention or expansion; and

3 Section 2. That § 61-5-29.3 be AMENDED:
4 61-5-29.3. The Governor's Office of Economic Development shall provide a written
5 report to either the Joint Committee on Appropriations or the Interim Committee on
6 Appropriations, the House Committee on State Affairs, the House Committee on
7 Commerce and Energy, the Senate Committee on State Affairs, Senate Committee on
8 Commerce and Energy, and the Government Operations and Audit Committee on a
9 biannual an annual basis the following for each award or grant made from the fund:
10 (1) The name of the recipient and the amount of the award or grant;
11 (2) The location of the recipient;
12 (3) The research or economic development purpose being funded;
13 (4) The measures being used to determine the economic impact of the award or
14 grant;and
15 (5) The number of jobs created or retained, if any;
16 (6) The balance of the fund at the time of reporting;
17 (7) The status of the research or economic development purpose; and
18 (8) For awards or grants made more than two years prior to the report, any projection
19 of jobs created or retained in this state by the recipient of the award or grant
20 resulting from the research or economic development purpose since the award or
21 grant was made.
22 Upon the request of the chair of a committee receiving the report, pursuant to this
23 section, the commissioner of the Governor's Office of Economic Development shall appear
24 before the committee, to answer questions that the members of the committee may have
25 on the report.
26 Section 3. That a NEW SECTION be added to chapter 61-5:
27 If an applicant for an award from the employer's investment in South Dakota's
28 future fund is a business, the applicant must submit a business plan to the Governor's
29 Office of Economic Development before the Governor may award the applicant any
30 moneys from the fund. The applicant's business plan must include:
31 (1) The goods or services to be offered by the applicant;
32 (2) A description of:
33 (a) The types of jobs required to offer the goods or services;

2 (c) The compensation package to be offered to individuals employed to perform
3 each type of job;
4 (3) The accounting practices employed by the business; and
5 (4) Other private or public funding received by the applicant.
6 Section 4. That a NEW SECTION be added to chapter 61-5:
7 Moneys from the employer's investment in South Dakota's future fund may be
8 awarded only to reimburse the actual cost of the research or economic development
9 project.
10 Before any moneys may be expended from the fund, the applicant shall sign an
11 agreement with the Governor's Office of Economic Development. If the award from the
12 fund exceeds one million dollars, the applicant must provide an itemized invoice of all
13 expenditures made by the applicant required in furtherance of the project and
14 documentation demonstrating proof of payment before any moneys may be expended
15 from the fund.
16 Any agreement executed pursuant to this section is open to public inspection and
17 must be posted on the state's website.
18 Section 5. That a NEW SECTION be added to chapter 61-5:
19 Before the Governor may approve the award of moneys from the employer's
20 investment in South Dakota's future fund, the Governor's Office of Economic Development
21 shall provide a detailed recommendation for the award to the Governor. Upon receiving
22 approval for the award from the Governor, the commissioner of the Governor's Office of
23 Economic Development and the applicant must sign the agreement required pursuant to
24 section 4 of this Act.
25 Each agreement for an award provided pursuant to this section is open to public
26 inspection and must be posted on the state's website. Any information in the
27 recommendation that constitutes trade secrets or other sensitive or privileged information
28 is not open to public inspection. Nothing in this section may be construed to repeal or
29 otherwise modify any confidentiality requirements found in state law.
