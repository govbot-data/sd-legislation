26.883.10 101st Legislative Session 1160
2026 South Dakota Legislature
House Bill 1160
Introduced by: Representative Goodwin
1 An Act to repeal the medical marijuana oversight committee.
2 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF SOUTH DAKOTA:
3 Section 1. That § 34-20G-92 be REPEALED.
4 The Executive Board of the Legislative Research Council shall appoint an oversight
5 committee consisting of:
6 (1) Two members of the Senate;
7 (2) Two members of the House of Representatives;
8 (3) One physician licensed in accordance with chapter 36-4;
9 (4) One physician assistant licensed in accordance with chapter 36-4A;
10 (5) One certified nurse practitioner licensed in accordance with chapter 36-9A;
11 (6) One chief of police for a municipality having a population in excess of fifty-
12 thousand, or a representative of the police department designated by the chief;
13 (7) One sheriff of a county or a representative of the sheriff's office designated by the
14 sheriff;
15 (8) One professional counselor licensed in accordance with chapter 36-32 or one
16 addiction counselor licensed in accordance with chapter 36-34; and
17 (9) One qualifying patient.
18 Each appointee shall serve for a term of two years and may be reappointed.
19 Section 2. That § 34-20G-92.1 be REPEALED.
20 Beginning in 2023, and every two years thereafter, the oversight committee shall
21 select from among itself one legislator to serve as the chair and one legislator, from the
22 opposite chamber, to serve as the vice chair.
23 Beginning in 2025, the legislators selected to serve as the chair and vice chair may
24 not be from the same chamber as their immediate predecessors.
Underscores indicate new language.
Overstrikes indicate deleted language.

1 Section 3. That § 34-20G-93 be REPEALED.
2 The oversight committee shall meet at least two times per year for the purpose of
3 evaluating and making recommendations to the Legislature and the department
4 regarding:
5 (1) The ability of qualifying patients in all areas of the state to obtain timely access to
6 high-quality medical cannabis;
7 (2) The effectiveness of the dispensaries and cultivation facilities, individually and
8 together, in serving the needs of qualifying patients, including the provision of
9 educational and support services by dispensaries, the reasonableness of their
10 prices, whether they are generating any complaints or security problems, and the
11 sufficiency of the number operating to serve the state's registered qualifying
12 patients;
13 (3) The effectiveness of the cannabis testing facilities, including whether a sufficient
14 number are operating;
15 (4) The sufficiency of the regulatory and security safeguards contained in this chapter
16 and adopted by the department to ensure that access to and use of cannabis
17 cultivated is provided only to cardholders;
18 (5) Any recommended additions or revisions to the department regulations or this
19 chapter, including recommendations relating to security, safe handling, labeling,
20 and nomenclature;
21 (6) Any research studies regarding health effects of medical cannabis for patients; and
22 (7) Any medical and clinical aspects of the medical cannabis program.
23 The oversight committee shall ensure that it seeks relevant input from qualifying
24 patients; designated caregivers; pharmacists; school boards and administrators; parents;
25 municipal representatives; state agencies, including the Department of Health, the South
26 Dakota Division of Criminal Investigation, and the Department of Public Safety; and
27 medical cannabis establishments.
