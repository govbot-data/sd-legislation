26.676.10 101st Legislative Session SCR604
2026 South Dakota Legislature
Senate Concurrent Resolution 604
SENATE ENGROSSED
Introduced by: Senator Voita
1 A CONCURRENT RESOLUTION urging the people of the state of South Dakota to seek
2 the Lord Most High for His healing presence and mercy upon South Dakota.
3 WHEREAS, we know that from the earliest days of the United States, the men and women
4 who worked to build up our nation trusted in the all-powerful and most merciful hand of
5 Providence to bless our land and guide their work with His wisdom; and
6 WHEREAS, we know that these men and women founded the United States upon the
7 Judeo-Christian values articulated by Sacred Scripture, among which are the beliefs that
8 government is established by God to safeguard the rights of the members of the community
9 over which it watches, that we are to love our neighbors as we ourselves have each been
10 loved by God, and that each human person has been created in the image and likeness of
11 God; and
12 WHEREAS, we hold, as John Adams wrote to the Massachusetts Militia on October 11,
13 1798, "Our Constitution was made only for a moral and religious People. It is wholly
14 inadequate to the government of any other"; and
15 WHEREAS, Article VIII, § 1 of the South Dakota Constitution affirms that the health of a
16 state depends on the virtue of its people; and
17 WHEREAS, the official motto of the State of South Dakota is "Under God, the People Rule";
18 and
19 WHEREAS, we hold that only a continual submission to the sovereignty of the Almighty
20 and wholly Righteous God, joined with an unyielding commitment to uphold the principles of
21 the Constitutions of the United States and the State of South Dakota, will allow us to protect
22 the liberties that have been given to us by the Almighty, from all forces that are intent on
23 advancing an agenda that seeks to turn us away from rightly loving God and our neighbors;
24 and

26.676.10 3 SCR604
1 WHEREAS, throughout the history of our nation, our leaders have repeatedly called upon
2 the people to return to Almighty God in a spirit of humility and penance, and beg the Father's
3 favor and mercy; and
4 WHEREAS, in a proclamation calling for a time of national fasting, John Adams wrote, "As
5 the safety and prosperity of nations ultimately and essentially depend on the protection and
6 the blessing of Almighty God; and the national acknowledgement of this truth is not only an
7 indispensable duty which the People owe to Him, but a duty whose natural influence is
8 favorable to the promotion of that Morality and Piety, without which social Happiness cannot
9 exist nor the Blessings of a Free Government be enjoyed; and as this Duty, at all times
10 incumbent, is so especially in seasons of Difficulty or of Danger, when existing or threatening
11 Calamities, the just Judgements of God against prevalent Iniquity, are a loud call to
12 Repentance and Reformation…Under these considerations it has appeared to me that the Duty
13 of imploring the Mercy and Benediction of Heaven on our Country demands, at this time, a
14 special attention from its inhabitants”; and
15 WHEREAS, without abdicating any responsibility for the actions that we take and
16 acknowledging that the government of our state and nation have the responsibility to govern
17 justly, we understand that none of our actions have the ability to come to a good end if the
18 Lord does not favor and bless our undertaking; and
19 WHEREAS, we hold that "Vain is the builder’s toil, if the house is not of the Lord’s building;
20 vainly the guard keeps watch, if the city has not the Lord for its guardian" (Ps. 127:1); and
21 WHEREAS, we join the psalmist's prayer to our own and say "Let the glorious majesty of
22 the Lord our God be upon us: prosper thou the work of our hands upon us; yea, prosper thou
23 the work of our hands" (Psalm 90:17);
24 NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED, by the Senate of the One Hundred First Legislature
25 of the State of South Dakota, the House of Representatives concurring therein, that each
26 year, the month of July be recognized as a time of prayer and fasting in South Dakota; and
27 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that through our acts of prayer and fasting, we seek the
28 continued blessings of the Lord Most High upon South Dakota, and affirm that He, as our
29 Creator and God, the King of All Glory, holds all authority, both in Heaven and on earth, to
30 judge and bless nations, states, and peoples as He sees fit; and

26.676.10 4 SCR604
1 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that with a humble spirit and a contrite heart, we recognize
2 our sins and our weaknesses, both individually and as a people, and beg Him for His
3 forgiveness and His strength; and
4 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that we ask the Lord Jesus to remove from us all corruption,
5 violence, and addiction, to heal all hearts and minds, to restore marriages and fortify families,
6 and to bring a righteous vision and a unity of purpose to all South Dakotans; and
7 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that we ask the Holy Spirit, the Comforter and the Paraclete,
8 to move within the halls of government, classrooms, businesses, places of worship, and
9 homes of this state to bring to the people of the State of South Dakota a spirit of healing,
10 love, joy, peace, patience, gentleness, kindness, goodness, faithfulness, and temperance;
11 and
12 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that we call upon all those who are physically able and
13 spiritually inclined to join us annually during the month of July, for a time of prayer and
14 fasting, depriving ourselves of those temporary physical comforts so that we may be
15 awakened to our need and hunger for God, humbling ourselves before Him, prayerfully
16 seeking His face, asking for His forgiveness, forsaking all wickedness, and begging Him to
17 bestow His healing, blessing, grace, and mercy upon us, so that we, our communities, our
18 state, and our nation will be transformed into a people fit to be His own.
