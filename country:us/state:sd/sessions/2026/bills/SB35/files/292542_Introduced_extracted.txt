26.407.9 101st Legislative Session 35
2026 South Dakota Legislature
Senate Bill 35
Introduced by: The Chair of the Committee on Military and Veterans Affairs at the request of the Department of
Veterans Affairs
1 An Act to revise the process for requesting a veteran's certificate of discharge from
2 a county register of deeds.
3 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF SOUTH DAKOTA:
4 Section 1. That § 33A-2-8 be AMENDED:
5 33A-2-8. The provisions of subdivision 43-28-2(7) apply to any certificate of
6 discharge of any person who is a veteran as defined in § 33A-2-1. The certificate shall be
7 recorded without charge and a certified copy of each version of the veteran's discharge,
8 excluding any copy that does not reflect the veteran's character of discharge, shall be
9 furnished to the person named in the certificate or the person's dependents without charge
10 if requested for the purpose of presenting or prosecuting a claim for compensation or
11 pension. Otherwise, a discharge document recorded by the recorder or a designated
12 official may be made available only to the veteran, the veteran's parents, the veteran's
13 next of kin, the veteran's legal representative, a county veterans service officer, a
14 nationally accredited veterans' organization service officer, the Department of Veterans
15 Affairs, or the veteran's designee. Any person requesting a discharge document shall
16 complete a form containing a statement specifying the person's eligibility to receive the
17 document based upon this section. The Department of Veterans Affairs shall provide the
18 forms to each county register of deeds
19 A veteran may record, with a county register of deeds, any certificate of discharge
20 from service in the armed forces of the United States or the National Guard, without
21 charge.
22 The county register of deeds shall provide a certified copy of the veteran’s
23 certificate of discharge, without charge, upon request from:
24 (1) The veteran;
25 (2) A representative of the Department of Veterans Affairs;
26 (3) An individual designated by the veteran to have access to the certificate;

1 (4) The veteran's legal representative;
2 (5) A veterans' service officer that is:
3 (a) Employed by a county in this state, pursuant to § 33A-1-22; or
4 (b) Appointed by an Indian tribe, pursuant to § 33A-1-29;
5 (6) A veterans service organization representative, accredited by the United States
6 Department of Veterans Affairs; or
7 (7) The veteran's next of kin, if the veteran is deceased.
8 This section does not apply to a version of a veteran's certificate of discharge that
9 does not display the veteran's type of discharge.
10 An individual requesting a certified copy of a veteran's certificate of discharge shall
11 submit to the register of deeds a form, provided by the Department of Veterans Affairs,
12 that discloses the individual's eligibility to be issued the certified copy pursuant to this
13 section.
