10 SECTION 1. TITLE AND PURPOSE
11 This statute shall be known and cited as the Athletic Trainer Compact. The purposes
12 of this compact are to expand the mobility of Athletic Training practice and improve public
13 access to services by providing qualified Licensed Athletic Trainers the ability to practice
14 in other Member States. This compact preserves the regulatory authority of States to
15 protect public health and safety through the current system of State licensure.
16 This compact is designed to achieve the following objectives:
17 A. Increase public access to Athletic Training and enhance continuity of care by
18 providing for the mutual recognition of other Licenses issued by Member States;
19 B. Provide an additional streamlined opportunity for interstate practice by Licensed
20 Athletic Trainers who meet compact uniform requirements;
21 C. Promote mobility and workforce development by eliminating the necessity for
22 Licenses in multiple States by providing for the mutual recognition of other Licenses
23 issued by member States;
24 D. Reduce administrative burdens on Licensed Athletic Trainers and Member States;
25 E. Enhance the States' ability to protect the public's health and safety;

1 F. Encourage the cooperation of Member States in regulating interstate practice of
2 Licensed Athletic Trainers;
3 G. Support relocating Active Military Members and their spouses;
4 H. Enhance the exchange of licensure, investigative, and disciplinary information
5 among Member States;
6 I. Allow for the use of telehealth to facilitate increased access to Athletic Training
7 services;
8 J. Support the uniformity of Licensed Athletic Trainer licensure requirements
9 throughout the States;
10 K. Affirm the authority of all Member States to hold a Licensed Athletic Trainer
11 accountable for abiding by the Scope of Practice in the State in which the patient
12 is located at the time; and
13 L. Require adherence to the Model Compact Language in order to promote uniformity
14 and ensure that all Member States have accepted and are mutually obligated to
15 the same terms.

24 SECTION 6. ACTIVE MILITARY MEMBERS OR THEIR SPOUSES
25 An Active Military Member or the member's spouse shall not be required to pay a
26 fee to the Commission for a Compact Privilege. If a Member State chooses to charge a
27 Member State fee, it may choose to charge a reduced fee or no fee to an Active Military
28 Member or the member's spouse for a Compact Privilege.

34 Investigative Information of a Remote State, so long as the Member State follows
35 its own procedures for imposing Adverse Action.
