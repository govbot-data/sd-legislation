13 the maximal physical and mental functioning of the individual in his or her daily
14 pursuits. The practice of occupational therapy includes consultation, evaluation,
15 and treatment of individuals whose abilities to cope with the tasks of living are
16 threatened or impaired by developmental deficits, the aging process, learning
17 disabilities, poverty and cultural differences, physical injury or disease,

19 therapy services include such treatment techniques as task-oriented activities to
20 prevent or correct physical or emotional deficits or to minimize the disabling effect
21 of these deficits in the life of the individual; such evaluation techniques as
22 assessment of sensory integration and motor abilities, assessment of development
23 of self-care and feeding, activities and capacity for independence, assessment of
24 the physical capacity for prevocational and work tasks, assessment of play and
25 leisure performance, and appraisal of living areas for the handicapped; physical
26 agent modalities limited to the upper extremities to enhance physical functional
