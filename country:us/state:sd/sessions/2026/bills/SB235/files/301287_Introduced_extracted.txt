26.991.18 101st Legislative Session 235
2026 South Dakota Legislature
Senate Bill 235
Introduced by: Senator Crabtree
1 An Act to protect residents from increased utility costs and utility shortages caused
2 by data centers, to clarify authority to regulate data centers, and to modify
3 provisions pertaining to the purchasing of goods and services by a data
4 center.
5 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF SOUTH DAKOTA:
6 Section 1. This Act is known as the "Data Center Bill of Rights for Citizens."
7 Section 2. That a NEW SECTION be added to chapter 10-45:
8 There are exempted from the provisions of this chapter, and the tax imposed by
9 it, the gross receipts from a purchase made by a data center, as defined in section 4 of
10 this Act.
11 Section 3. That a NEW SECTION be added to chapter 10-46:
12 There are exempted from the provisions of this chapter, and the tax imposed by
13 it, the gross receipts from a purchase made by a data center, as defined in section 4 of
14 this Act.
15 Section 4. That a NEW SECTION be added to a NEW CHAPTER in title 49:
16 For purposes of this chapter, "data center" means a centralized repository for the
17 dissemination, management, processing, and storage of electronic data and information.
18 Section 5. That a NEW SECTION be added to a NEW CHAPTER in title 49:
19 The operator of a data center shall pay for all costs associated with the provision
20 of electricity to the data center.
21 A provider of electricity may not:

7 Section 6. That a NEW SECTION be added to a NEW CHAPTER in title 49:
8 The state may not preempt or otherwise limit the authority of the governing body
9 of a county, municipality, or other political subdivision to adopt ordinances and resolutions
10 limiting, prohibiting, or otherwise regulating the construction, development, or operation
11 of data centers.
12 Section 7. That a NEW SECTION be added to a NEW CHAPTER in title 49:
13 Prior to commencing operation in this state, the operator of a data center shall
14 provide notice of projected water consumption to each local water provider in the area in
15 which the data center is proposed to be located. Each water provider receiving notice
16 pursuant to this section shall determine whether the data center's projected water
17 consumption is compatible with the local water supply and issue that determination in
18 writing to the operator. The operator shall present a copy of each notice and determination
19 to the Board of Water Management.
20 If the board receives a determination from each applicable water provider that the
21 data center's projected water consumption is compatible with the local water supply, the
22 board must issue, to the operator and the general public, a written statement that the
23 operator has satisfied the requirements of this section.
24 Notwithstanding the board's issuance of a written statement pursuant to this
25 section, the data center may not consume an amount of the local water supply exceeding
26 limits established by order of the board, after allocation to residential and essential public
27 services. In establishing allocation limits under this section, the board shall seek to ensure
28 water availability and prevent disproportionate use of water by data centers.
29 Section 8. That a NEW SECTION be added to a NEW CHAPTER in title 49:

31 to this chapter, shall, at the time and in the manner prescribed by the Board of Water
