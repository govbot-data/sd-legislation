1 10-6-128. The secretary of revenue, upon notifying the Agricultural Land
2 Assessment Implementation and Oversight Advisory Task Force, the department may
3 contract with one or more qualified entities for the purpose of creating or maintaining a
4 database to determine the agricultural income value of agricultural land by county,
5 consisting of eight consecutive years of cropland data and noncropland data. The data for
6 the year representing the highest agricultural income value among the eight years and
7 the year representing the lowest agricultural income value among the eight years shall
8 must be discarded from the database and the remaining six years averaged for valuation
9 purposes. For each assessment year, the first year of the eight-year data period shall be
10 the year that is nine years less than prior to the assessment year.
11 The cropland data must include acres planted, acres harvested, yield per acre, and
12 statewide crop prices. The noncropland data must include cash rents and statewide calf
13 prices. The noncropland data may also include rangeland and pastureland animal unit
14 months per acre, rangeland and pastureland acres, and grazing season data.
15 Any A qualified entity contracting with the secretary of revenue the department
16 pursuant to this section shall provide the data for each county to the secretary of revenue
17 by June first of each year.
18 For purposes of this section, a qualified entity is a federal or state agency,
19 instrumentality, institution of higher learning, other federal or state authority, or private
20 entity with expertise in researching or evaluating land production capacity.
21 On or before March first of each year, the department shall provide a report to the
22 Taxation Committees in the Senate and the House of Representatives on the data compiled
23 pursuant to this section.

1 Section 4. That § 10-6-134 be REPEALED.
2 There is hereby established the Agricultural Land Assessment Implementation and
3 Oversight Advisory Task Force. The task force consists of the following fourteen members:
4 (1) The speaker of the House of Representatives shall appoint four members of the
5 House of Representatives. The speaker shall appoint members in a manner that is
6 proportional to a party's representation in the House of Representatives, except
7 the speaker shall appoint at least one member from the minority party in the House
8 of Representatives;
9 (2) The speaker of the House of Representatives shall appoint three members of the
10 general public, at least one of the members shall have an agricultural background
11 and at least one of the members shall have a business background;
12 (3) The president pro tempore of the Senate shall appoint four members of the Senate.
13 The president pro tempore shall appoint members in a manner that is proportional
14 to a party's representation in the Senate, except the president pro tempore shall
15 appoint at least one member from the minority party in the Senate; and
16 (4) The president pro tempore of the Senate shall appoint three members of the
17 general public, at least one of the members shall have an agricultural background
18 and at least one of the members shall have a business background.
19 The speaker of the House of Representatives and president pro tempore of the
20 Senate before the close of each regular session of the Legislature held in odd-numbered
21 years shall appoint members to the task force for a term of two years. If there is a vacancy
22 on the task force, the vacancy shall be filled in the same manner as the original
23 appointment.
24 The task force shall advise the department regarding the rules promulgated by the
25 department to administer the provisions concerning the assessment and taxation of
26 agricultural lands and shall review the implementation of the provisions of law concerning
27 the assessment and taxation of agricultural land. The task force shall report to the Senate
28 and House of Representatives and may submit a copy of its report to the Governor. The
29 task force may present draft legislation and policy recommendations to the Legislative
30 Research Council Executive Board.
31 Section 5. That § 10-6-135 be REPEALED.
