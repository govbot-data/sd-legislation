6 Section 1. That § 32-12-5.1 be AMENDED:
7 32-12-5.1. The Department of Public Safety may deny the issuance of a motor
8 vehicle operator's license, motorcycle operator's license, restricted minor's permit,
9 motorcycle restricted minor's permit, instruction permit, or motorcycle instruction permit
10 to any individual who has experienced convulsions, seizures, or blackouts, until the
11 individual has experienced a period of twelve months without any such episode. However,
12 upon only if the applicant has been diagnosed, by a licensed physician, as having a chronic
13 medical condition that could interfere with the operation of a motor vehicle. Any person
14 with a physical condition that would detract from the ability to safely operate a motor
15 vehicle must show, through examination by a personal physician, that the person has the
16 ability to do so. Upon receipt of a statement signed by the applicant that the applicant's
17 condition is adequately controlled by medication, the applicant is continuing to take
18 medication, and the applicant is under the care of a physician, the Department of Public
19 Safety may issue a temporary permit to the applicant. This temporary permit is subject
20 to the provisions of § 32-12-36 and is reviewable by the department every six months, or
21 until the applicant has gone a period of twelve months without any episode.
22 If the department has reasonable grounds to believe that an applicant's medical
23 condition may affect the safe operation of a motor vehicle or motorcycle, the department
24 may require the applicant to submit to the department the results of a medical
25 examination or physician's a licensed health care practitioner's certification attesting to
26 the applicant's fitness to operate a motor vehicle in order to receive the temporary permit.
