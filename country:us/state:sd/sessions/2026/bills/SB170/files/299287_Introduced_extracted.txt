26.254.11 101st Legislative Session 170
2026 South Dakota Legislature
Senate Bill 170
Introduced by: Senator Larson
1 An Act to require the provision of a notice to consumers, interacting with certain
2 chatbots or other human-simulating computer technologies that could
3 mislead or deceive the consumer.
4 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF SOUTH DAKOTA:
5 Section 1. That a NEW SECTION be added to chapter 37-24:
6 Except as otherwise provided in this section, a person may not engage in a
7 commercial transaction or trade practice with a consumer if:
8 (1) The transaction or practice requires the consumer to communicate with or interact
9 with a chatbot, an artificial intelligence agent, an avatar, or another form of
10 computer technology that engages in a textual or aural conversation; and
11 (2) The consumer could reasonably believe that the consumer is engaging with
12 human.
13 The prohibition set forth in this section does not apply if the consumer is notified,
14 in a clear and conspicuous fashion, at the outset of the transaction or practice, that the
15 consumer is not communicating with another human.
16 Section 2. That a NEW SECTION be added to chapter 37-24:
17 Except as otherwise provided in this section, either a consumer subjected to a
18 commercial transaction or trade practice that does not comply with section 1 of this Act,
19 or the attorney general, may bring a civil action against the violating person, except as
20 provided below, and may recover:
21 (1) Actual damages;
22 (2) Liquidated damages in the amount of one thousand dollars for each violation; or
23 (3) In the case of a class action, an amount not exceeding ten million dollars.
24 The prevailing party may also recover reasonable attorney's fees and costs.
