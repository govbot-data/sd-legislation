26.982.13 101st Legislative Session 222
2026 South Dakota Legislature
Senate Bill 222
Introduced by: Senator Karr
1 An Act to make an appropriation for eligible water, wastewater, storm water, and
2 nonpoint source projects and to declare an emergency.
3 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF SOUTH DAKOTA:
4 Section 1. There is appropriated $10,000,000 from the general fund, to the Department of
5 Agriculture and Natural Resources, for the purpose of providing grants for eligible water,
6 wastewater, storm water, and nonpoint source projects in accordance with the guidance
7 provided in section 2 of this Act.
8 Section 2. To be eligible for funding, projects must go through the Department of Agriculture
9 and Natural Resources by way of the Board of Water and Natural Resources and be included
10 in the state water plan. The following funding terms apply to eligible projects under this Act:
11 (1) Eligible applicants may receive a grant award limited to a per person project cost
12 based on the population served by the system or municipality, using the 2020
13 federal census numbers or system service numbers, as determined by the board.
14 The grant limit must apply to each applicant, regardless of the project cost or the
15 number of projects in need of funding, and must be determined as follows:
16 (a) Systems or municipalities with service populations of up to one thousand
17 may receive up to an eighty percent grant, with a maximum project cost
18 for determining the grant amount limited to nine thousand dollars per
19 person per applicant for all eligible projects considered for grants;
20 (b) Systems or municipalities with service populations with at least one
21 thousand and up to two thousand five hundred may receive up to a fifty
22 percent grant with a maximum project cost for determining the grant
23 amount limited to seven thousand dollars per person per applicant for all
24 eligible projects considered for grants; and
25 (c) Systems or municipalities with service populations greater than two
26 thousand five hundred may receive a thirty percent grant with a maximum
