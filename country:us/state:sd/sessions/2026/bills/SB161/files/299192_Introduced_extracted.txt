26.799.13 101st Legislative Session 161
2026 South Dakota Legislature
Senate Bill 161
Introduced by: Senator Pischke
1 An Act to repeal an exemption for certain health care facilities.
2 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF SOUTH DAKOTA:
3 Section 1. That § 10-1-47 be AMENDED:
4 10-1-47. The terms "United States Internal Revenue Code" and "Internal Revenue
5 Code" mean the United States Internal Revenue Code as amended and in effect on January
6 1, 2025. This section applies to §§ 10-4-9.1, 10-4-9.2, 10-4-9.3, 10-4-9.4, 10-4-39, 10-
7 6-157, and 10-43-10.1, and subdivisions 10-6A-1(7), 10-6B-1(5), 10-18A-1(6), 10-43-
8 10.3(6), and 10-45A-1(5).
9 Section 2. That § 10-4-11 be AMENDED:
10 10-4-11. If any property owned by any society or institution described in §§ 10-
11 4-9 to 10-4-9.3 10-4-9.2, inclusive, shall consist consists of hotel or residence property,
12 or any other class of retail business and such the property is used or owned primarily for
13 the purposes of revenue, and not for the primary object of such charitable, benevolent,
14 or religious society, it shall must be taxed as other property of the same class is taxed.
15 Section 3. That § 10-4-12 be AMENDED:
16 10-4-12. If property owned by any health care organization or charitable,
17 benevolent, or religious society described in § 10-4-9.4 and §§ 10-4-9 to 10-4-9.3 10-4-
18 9.4, inclusive, other than agricultural land, is used partly by such health care organization
19 or the charitable, benevolent, or religious society for health care, charitable, benevolent,
20 or religious purposes, and the remaining part is occupied, rented, or used for other than
21 health care, charitable, benevolent, or religious purposes, the portion of property that is
22 so occupied, rented, or used for other purposes, shall must be taxed as other property of
23 the same class is taxed.

7 Section 4. That § 10-4-9.3 be REPEALED.
8 Property owned by any corporation, organization, or society and used primarily for
9 human health care and health care related purposes is exempt from taxation. Such
10 corporation, organization, or society shall be nonprofit and recognized as an exempt
11 organization under section 501(c)(3) of the United States Internal Revenue Code, as
12 defined by § 10-1-47, and none of its assets may be available to any private interest. The
13 property shall be a health care facility licensed pursuant to chapter 34-12, orphanage,
14 mental health center or community support provider regulated under chapter 27A-5, or
15 camp. The facility shall admit all persons for treatment consistent with the facility's ability
16 to provide health care services required by the patient until the facility is filled to its
17 ordinary capacity and conform to all applicable regulations of and permit inspections by
18 the state as otherwise provided by law.
19 Section 5. That § 10-4-35 be REPEALED.
20 For the purposes of § 10-4-9.3, a "wellness center" is a health care facility if it links
21 on- and off-site medical consultants with physical activity staff and programs of health
22 and fitness facilities, can and does provide fitness testing and prescription exercise tailored
23 to specific individual needs and capabilities, and the property is used for rehabilitation
24 services or prescriptive exercise. For the purposes of § 10-4-12, the taxable percentage
25 of a wellness center shall be determined by computing the percentage of the total
26 membership that do not use the facility for physician prescribed rehabilitation or
27 prescriptive exercise.
