26.954.9 101st Legislative Session HCR6014
2026 South Dakota Legislature
House Concurrent Resolution
6014
Introduced by: Representative Goodwin
1 A CONCURRENT RESOLUTION honoring Custer State Park cabin owners and their
2 property rights.
3 WHEREAS, when Custer State Park was created, then Governor Vassey encouraged
4 private citizens to build cabins in the park to attract more visitors; and
5 WHEREAS, today there are thirty-seven privately owned cabins in the park, on land leased
6 from Custer State Park; and
7 WHEREAS, the cabin owners pay property tax on the land leased from Custer County, pay
8 their lease payments to Custer State Park, and contribute regularly to the needs of the park
9 through the Custer State Park Foundation; and
10 WHEREAS, most cabin owners' leases expire in a few years; and
11 WHEREAS, the recent wind storms in the park have damaged some of the cabins, which
12 has accentuated the need to create some certainty as to the future of the cabins in the park;
13 and
14 WHEREAS, the cabin owners have a long legacy with the park and consider themselves
15 guardians of the park and its heritage; and
16 WHEREAS, the cabin owners have property rights that need to be respected and honored,
17 and time is of the essence to address these issues; and
18 WHEREAS, the South Dakota Legislature can help these cabin owners by ensuring their
19 property interests are protected and their place in the legacy of the park and the state
20 continues;
Underscores indicate new language.
Overstrikes indicate deleted language.

26.954.9 2 HCR6014
1 NOW, THEREFORE, BE IT RESOLVED, by the House of Representatives of the One Hundred
2 First Legislature of the State of South Dakota, the Senate concurring therein, that the
3 Legislature endorses the cabin owners' property rights in Custer State Park, and their desire
4 to know their future in the park, and encourages the state to embrace the cabin owners as
5 important to the park's future; and
6 BE IT FURTHER RESOLVED, that the Governor and the Department of Game, Fish and
7 Parks be encouraged to create new leases with the cabin owners, so the cabin owners may
8 secure their future and enhance the park as quickly as possible.
