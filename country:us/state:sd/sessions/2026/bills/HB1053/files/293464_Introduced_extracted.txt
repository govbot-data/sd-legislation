26.248.17 2 1053
1 Notwithstanding § 34-20G-25.1, a terminal care facility shall permit a cardholder,
2 who is a patient or resident of the facility and has a terminal condition, to store and use
3 medical cannabis in the facility.
4 Prior to bringing medical cannabis on the facility's premises, the cardholder or the
5 cardholder's designated caregiver shall provide a copy of the cardholder's registry
6 identification card to the terminal care facility, and the cardholder's attending practitioner
7 shall indicate the use of medical cannabis in the cardholder's medical records.
8 A cardholder may not smoke or vape medical cannabis in a terminal care facility.
9 A terminal care facility shall establish policies and procedures for the
10 administration, storage, and use of medical cannabis within the facility.
11 This section does not apply to a cardholder receiving treatment in the emergency
12 care department of a hospital. Nothing in this section requires a terminal care facility, or
13 personnel working at the facility, to procure or administer medical cannabis to a patient.
14 Terms used in this section mean:
15 (1) "Terminal care facility," a hospital, inpatient hospice, or residential hospice, as
16 defined in § 34-12-1.1; and
17 (2) "Terminal condition" a medical condition that is diagnosed by a health care
18 practitioner and has a life prognosis of less than one year.
19 Section 3. That a NEW SECTION be added to chapter 34-20G:
20 A terminal care facility, as defined in section 2 of this Act, is not required to comply
21 with section 2 of this Act if any agency or department of the United States government:
22 (1) Initiates enforcement action against the terminal care facility related to the
23 facility's compliance with this chapter; or
24 (2) Issues a rule, or otherwise provides notification to the terminal care facility, that
25 expressly prohibits the use of medical cannabis in the facility.
