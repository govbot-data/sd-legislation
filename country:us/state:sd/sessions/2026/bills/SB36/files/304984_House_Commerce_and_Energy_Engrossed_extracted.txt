1 An Act to authorize certain utilities to establish wildfire mitigation plans and
2 associated liability limitations for wildfire damages.
3 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF SOUTH DAKOTA:
4 Section 1. That a NEW SECTION be added to a NEW CHAPTER in title 49:
5 Terms used in this chapter mean:
6 (1) "Approval authority," in the case of an electric cooperative, its board of directors,
7 or in the case of a municipal utility, its city council or city commission, as the case
8 may be;
9 (2) "Commission," the Public Utilities Commission of the State of South Dakota;
10 (3) "Economic loss," monetary loss, including losses resulting from medical expenses,
11 business interruption, loss of business, loss of use of property, and loss due to
12 death and burial costs;
13 (4) "Qualified utility," includes:
14 (a) Any electric utility defined in subdivision 49-34A-1(7); and
15 (b) Any person who is a wholesale generator of electricity and who operates
16 electrical transmission;
17 (5) "Wildfire," an unplanned, unwanted fire burning in this state that:
18 (a) May impact, damage, or cause harm, including to natural resources,
19 agricultural resources, homes, and property; or
20 (b) Threatens lives and safety; and
21 (6) "Wildfire mitigation plan," a plan submitted to and approved by the approval
22 authority or published by the commission in accordance with the requirements of
23 this chapter.

20 Section 3. That a NEW SECTION be added to a NEW CHAPTER in title 49:
21 A qualified utility with a published wildfire mitigation plan shall submit an annual
22 report detailing the qualified utility's compliance with its wildfire mitigation plan for the
23 previous calendar year to the commission or to its approval authority, no later than June
24 first of each year. The commission shall file and publish the report within sixty days of
25 receipt. The electric cooperative or municipal utility shall publish the report on its website
26 within sixty days of filing with the approval authority. The wildfire mitigation plan is valid
27 and current if it is on file and all annual reports are timely filed with the commission or
28 approval authority. The qualified utility shall file an update to the wildfire mitigation plan
29 at least once every two years.

1 A standard of strict liability may not be applied to a qualified utility in any cause of
2 action alleging that the qualified utility caused wildfire-related damages. A qualified
3 utility's facilities and activities may not be considered ultra-hazardous or abnormally
4 dangerous.
5 A plaintiff's sole remedy against a qualified utility for damages and losses resulting
6 from a wildfire is in this chapter.
7 Except as otherwise provided in this section, there is a rebuttable presumption in
8 any civil action that a valid and current wildfire mitigation plan is a reasonable and prudent
9 preparation for, and mitigation of, wildfire risk. A plaintiff may recover damages, as a
10 result of a wildfire from a qualified utility with a valid and current wildfire mitigation plan
11 only if the plaintiff proves one or both of the following by a preponderance of the evidence:
12 (1) The qualified utility failed to substantially comply with an essential element of a
13 valid and current wildfire mitigation plan, and that failure was the actual and
14 proximate cause of the damages to the plaintiff. For purposes of this subdivision,
15 a qualified utility is deemed to have substantially complied with its wildfire
16 mitigation plan if the qualified utility attempted to comply with the plan but was
17 denied or delayed access to a right-of-way after the qualified utility requested
18 access to the right-of-way to perform vegetation management or fire mitigation
19 work in accordance with the plan; or
20 (2) The qualified utility acted with malice or criminal intent, and the qualified utility's
21 malicious action or criminal intent was the actual and proximate cause of the
22 damages to the plaintiff.
23 A civil action filed against a qualified utility for damages caused by wildfire may
24 only be brought within four years after the date of the initial ignition of the wildfire.
25 A plaintiff in a civil action filed against a qualified utility for damages caused by
26 wildfire may recover economic losses and property damages but only under the conditions
27 of this chapter. The plaintiff may not recover noneconomic losses unless the plaintiff
28 suffered death, visible bodily injury, smoke inhalation, or a medically verifiable bodily
29 injury from a wildfire.
30 The plaintiff may not recover punitive damages unless it is proven by clear and
31 convincing evidence that the qualified utility engaged in willful and wanton misconduct
32 and the qualified utility's willful and wanton misconduct was the actual and proximate
33 cause of damages to the plaintiff.
34 The amount of damages recoverable for damage to real property, or buildings and
35 fixtures to real property, is the lesser of the cost to restore the property to the property's
