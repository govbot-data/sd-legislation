1 An Act to authorize utilities to establish wildfire mitigation plans and associated
2 liability limitations for wildfire damages.
3 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF SOUTH DAKOTA:
4 Section 1. That a NEW SECTION be added to a NEW CHAPTER in title 49:
5 Terms used in this chapter mean:
6 (1) "Approval authority," in the case of an electric cooperative, its board of directors,
7 or in the case of a municipal utility, its city council or city commission, as the case
8 may be;
9 (2) "Commission," the Public Utilities Commission of the State of South Dakota;
10 (3) "Economic loss," monetary loss, including losses resulting from medical expenses,
11 business interruption, loss of business, loss of use of property, and loss due to
12 death and burial costs;
13 (4) "Qualified utility," includes:
14 (a) Any electric utility defined in subdivision 49-34A-1(7); and
15 (b) Any person who is a wholesale generator of electricity and who operates
16 electrical transmission;
17 (5) "Wildfire," an unplanned, unwanted fire burning in this state that:
18 (a) May impact, damage, or cause harm, including to natural resources,
19 agricultural resources, homes, and property; or
20 (b) Threatens lives and safety; and
21 (6) "Wildfire mitigation plan," a plan submitted to and approved or published by the
22 approval authority in accordance with the requirements of this chapter.
23 Section 2. That a NEW SECTION be added to a NEW CHAPTER in title 49:
24 A qualified utility that is not an electric cooperative or municipal utility may submit
25 a wildfire mitigation plan to the commission for filing and publishing. The commission shall

18 Section 3. That a NEW SECTION be added to a NEW CHAPTER in title 49:
19 A qualified utility with a published wildfire mitigation plan shall submit an annual
20 report detailing the qualified utility's compliance with its wildfire mitigation plan for the
21 previous calendar year to the commission or to its approval authority, no later than June
22 first of each year. The commission shall file and publish the report within sixty days of
23 receipt. The electric cooperative or municipal utility shall publish the report on its website
24 within sixty days of filing with the approval authority. The wildfire mitigation plan is valid
25 and current if it is on file and all annual reports are timely filed with the commission or
26 approval authority. The qualified utility shall file an update to the wildfire mitigation plan
27 at least once every five years.

29 A wildfire mitigation plan must include, at a minimum:
30 (1) A description of areas within the service territory or facility locations of the qualified
31 utility, which may be subject to a heightened risk of wildfire;
32 (2) A description of the procedures or strategies, standards, and time frames that the
33 qualified utility will use to inspect and operate its infrastructure;

21 Section 5. That a NEW SECTION be added to a NEW CHAPTER in title 49:
22 The commission may authorize a public utility as defined in subdivision 49-34A-
23 1(12) to recover in rates all prudently incurred investments and expenditures, including
24 the costs of capital, made to implement an approved wildfire mitigation plan.
25 Cost recovery for wildfire mitigation plan implementation may be allowed by the
26 commission, notwithstanding any settlement agreement provisions that restrict a public
27 utility's ability to seek rate relief.
28 Section 6. That a NEW SECTION be added to a NEW CHAPTER in title 49:
29 A standard of strict liability may not be applied to a qualified utility in any cause of
30 action alleging that the qualified utility caused wildfire-related damages. A qualified
31 utility's facilities and activities may not be considered ultra-hazardous or abnormally
32 dangerous.

1 A plaintiff's sole remedy against a qualified utility for damages and losses resulting
2 from a wildfire is in this chapter.
3 Except as otherwise provided in this section, there is a rebuttable presumption in
4 any civil action that a valid and current wildfire mitigation plan is a reasonable and prudent
5 preparation for, and mitigation of, wildfire risk. A plaintiff may recover damages from a
6 qualified utility with a valid and current wildfire mitigation plan, as a result of a wildfire,
7 only if the plaintiff proves one or more of the following by a preponderance of the
8 evidence:
9 (1) The qualified utility failed to substantially comply with an essential element of a
10 valid and current wildfire mitigation plan, and that failure was the actual and
11 proximate cause of the damages to the plaintiff. For purposes of this subdivision,
12 a qualified utility is deemed to have substantially complied with its wildfire
13 mitigation plan if the qualified utility attempted to comply with the plan but was
14 denied or delayed access to a right-of-way after the qualified utility requested
15 access to the right-of-way to perform vegetation management or fire mitigation
16 work in accordance with the plan; or
17 (2) The qualified utility acted with malice or criminal intent, and the qualified utility's
18 malicious action or criminal intent was the actual and proximate cause of the
19 damages to the plaintiff.
20 A civil action filed against a qualified utility for damages caused by wildfire may
21 only be brought within three years after the date of the initial ignition of the wildfire.
22 A plaintiff in a civil action filed against a qualified utility for damages caused by
23 wildfire may recover economic losses and property damages but only under the conditions
24 of this chapter. The plaintiff may not recover noneconomic losses unless the plaintiff
25 suffered death or visible bodily injury in the form of a burn from a wildfire.
26 The plaintiff may not recover punitive damages unless it is proven by clear and
27 convincing evidence that the qualified utility acted with malice or criminal intent and the
28 qualified utility's malicious action or criminal intent was the actual and proximate cause of
29 damages to the plaintiff.
30 The amount of damages recoverable for damage to real or personal property is the
31 lesser of the cost to restore the property to the property's pre-wildfire condition or the
32 difference between the fair market value of the property immediately before the wildfire
33 and the fair market value of the property after the wildfire.
34 Nothing in this section may be construed to limit any defenses that a qualified
35 utility may be entitled to raise in a civil action for damages caused by wildfire.
