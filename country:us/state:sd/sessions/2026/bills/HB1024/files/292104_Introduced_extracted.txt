1 An Act to modify requirements for ambulance operators.
2 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF SOUTH DAKOTA:
3 Section 1. That § 32-17-42 be AMENDED:
4 32-17-42. A motor vehicle may be equipped with blue lights if it is:
5 (1) Authorized by the Department of Transportation;
6 (2) A tow truck or wrecker as defined in § 32-17-10; or
7 (3) Operated by:
8 (a) A member of a fire department or search and rescue unit; or
9 (b) An ambulance operator certified in accordance with chapter 36-4B who
10 meets the requirements of section 4 of this Act.
11 This provision does not relieve the driver of the vehicle from the duty to drive with
12 due regard for the safety of all persons using the street, nor does it protect the driver
13 from the consequence of a reckless disregard for the safety of others.
14 No person may use a blue light unless the person is authorized by this chapter and
15 is operating a Department of Transportation vehicle or a county highway vehicle in
16 performance of winter highway maintenance duties, or the person is actually en route to
17 the scene of a fire or other emergency requiring the person's services and the person has
18 been authorized in writing to use a blue light. Authorization for use of a blue light in
19 accordance with this chapter may be given by the Department of Transportation, the
20 highway patrol, a county highway superintendent, the chief of a fire department, the
21 coordinator of a search and rescue unit, or the director of an ambulance service, and only
22 to members of the department or service who are in good standing.
23 The use of a blue light in violation of this section is a Class 2 misdemeanor.

13 Section 3. That § 34-11-12 be AMENDED:
14 34-11-12. Each ambulance run must include:
15 (1) One emergency medical technician or one advanced life support personnel,
16 licensed pursuant to chapter 36-4B; and
17 (2) One ambulance operator certified in accordance with chapter 36-4B who meets the
18 requirements of section 4 of this Act.
19 Section 4. That a NEW SECTION be added to chapter 34-11:
20 An ambulance operator must be eighteen years or older, possess a driver's license,
21 and:
22 (1) Be licensed as emergency medical services personnel in accordance with chapter
23 36-4B;
24 (2) Be a law enforcement officer, as defined in § 23-3-27; or
25 (3) Provide documentation to the ambulance service that the individual:
26 (a) Has completed training in cardiopulmonary resuscitation offered by a state
27 or national organization and approved by the medical or program director;
28 and
29 (b) Has completed an emergency vehicle operator course, which consists of at
30 least sixteen hours of instruction, offered by a state or national
31 organization, and approved by the medical or program director.
32 The ambulance service shall maintain the documentation required by this section
33 and shall provide it to the department, upon request.

1 36-4B-31. The board may deny the issuance or renewal of a license or
2 certification, and may suspend or revoke a license or certification issued under this
3 chapter, if the individual:
4 (1) Procures or attempts to procure a license or certification through fraud, deceit,
5 misrepresentation, or in violation of the provisions of this chapter and rules
6 promulgated thereunder;
7 (2) Is unable to perform the skills for which the individual is licensed;
8 (3) Has engaged in unprofessional or dishonorable conduct, as defined in § 36-4-30;
9 (4) Has been convicted of a felony that:
10 (a) Has a direct bearing upon the individual's ability to serve the public in a
11 capacity certified or licensed by this chapter; or
12 (b) Has been convicted of a crime that requires the individual to register as a
13 sex offender in any state;
14 (5) Has had the individual's National Registry of Emergency Technicians or other health
15 care certification or license encumbered for any reason;
16 (6) Provides emergency medical services without authorization from a physician,
17 physician assistant, or nurse practitioner; or
18 (7) Fails to respond to an emergency while on call through willful disregard and not by
19 a good-faith error or circumstances beyond the individual's control.
