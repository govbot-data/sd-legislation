Introduced by: Representative Reimer
1 An Act to modify provisions pertaining to the submission of a nominating petition.
2 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF SOUTH DAKOTA:
3 Section 1. That § 9-13-7 be AMENDED:
4 9-13-7. No candidate for elective municipal office may be nominated unless the
5 candidate files a nominating petition with the municipal finance officer no later than five
6 p.m. on the Tuesday seventy days before the date of the election. A petition is considered
7 filed if it is mailed by registered mail by five p.m. on the last day to file a petition local
8 time, on the third Tuesday of March, for an election held on the first Tuesday after the
9 first Monday in June, or before five p.m. local time, on the third Tuesday of August for an
10 election held on the first Tuesday after the first Monday in November.
11 The petition must be on the form prescribed by the State Board of Elections and
12 must contain:
13 (1) The name of the candidate;
14 (2) The candidate's residential address;
15 (3) The candidate's mailing address, if applicable; and
16 (4) The office the candidate seeks.
17 If an individual who signs a petition lives within a second-class or third-class
18 municipality, the individual may give the individual's post office box number in lieu of a
19 street address.
20 The finance officer may only accept a nominating petition that is on the prescribed
21 form and was circulated and submitted pursuant to the provisions of this chapter and
22 chapter 12-6. The municipal finance officer or clerk shall verify by signature that the
23 nominating petition contains the minimum number of signatures of registered voters
24 within the municipality or ward, and that the candidate is a registered voter within the
25 municipality or ward. Upon verification, the filing of a nominating petition constitutes
26 nomination.

1 Section 2. That § 9-13-7.1 be AMENDED:
2 9-13-7.1. Any person who has filed a nominating petition pursuant to § 9-13-7
3 may withdraw from the nomination by a written request, signed by the person and
4 properly acknowledged and filed with the finance officer of the municipality. The name of
5 a withdrawn or deceased candidate may not be printed on the ballot if the candidate
6 withdraws or dies no later than five p.m. on the deadline day for filing nominating
7 petitions. If the withdrawal or death of a candidate occurs at any time prior to five p.m.
8 on the deadline day for filing nominating petitions results in there being no contest on the
9 ballot, that ballot need not be voted. In addition, if that contest constitutes the only ballot
10 to be voted upon, then the election shall be canceled by the official in charge of the election
11 and the unopposed candidate shall be issued a certificate of election.
12 Any candidate who has filed a nominating petition pursuant to § 9-13-7 may
13 withdraw from the nomination by making a written request under oath. The candidate
14 shall file the request with the municipal finance officer. The finance officer may not print
15 the name of a candidate if the candidate withdraws or dies before five p.m. local time, on
16 the first Thursday after the deadline for filing a nominating petition.
17 If the death or withdrawal of a candidate at any time before the withdrawal
18 deadline results in the number of nominating petitions filed for the vacant position to be
19 equal to or less than the number of vacancies, the contest may not be placed on the ballot,
20 and the finance officer must issue a certificate of election to the unopposed candidate. If
21 that contest is the only contest to be voted upon, the finance officer must cancel the
22 election.
23 Section 3. That § 9-13-37 be AMENDED:
24 9-13-37. Any other provision of this chapter notwithstanding, the governing body

26 conjunction with the regular June primary election or the regular November general
27 election. The expenses and governmental responsibilities of a combined election must be
28 shared in a manner agreed upon by the governing body of the municipality and the board
29 of county commissioners involved.
30 A nominating petition may not be circulated for signatures more than four months
31 before the election before January first, for an election held on the first Tuesday after the
32 first Monday in June, or June first, for an election held on the first Tuesday after the first
33 Monday in November.
