8 Section 7. That a NEW SECTION be added to a NEW CHAPTER in title 12:
9 The office of presidential elector becomes vacant if the elector:
10 (1) Dies;
11 (2) Resigns;
12 (3) Fails to qualify, as provided by law;
13 (4) Fails to appear at the designated time and place of the meeting;
14 (5) Refuses to vote;
15 (6) Attempts to cast a blank ballot; or
16 (7) Except as provided for in section 8 of this Act, attempts to vote for any candidate
17 other than the candidate whose slate of electors received the highest number of
18 votes in the state's general election.
19 If a presidential elector casts a ballot that results in the office of elector becoming
20 vacant, the ballot may not be accepted or counted. An elector may resign the office of
21 presidential elector by submitting a letter of resignation to the secretary of state.
22 Section 8. That a NEW SECTION be added to a NEW CHAPTER in title 12:
23 If, between the date of the general election and the date designated by Congress
24 for the election of the president and vice president of the United States, the candidate for
25 the president or vice president whose slate of electors received the highest number of
26 votes in the state's general election withdraws, dies, or, because of a permanent disability,
27 becomes unable to discharge the powers and duties of the office to which the candidate
28 is elected, each presidential elector from this state must vote for the replacement
29 candidate nominated according to the bylaws of the party to which the previous candidate
30 belonged.
