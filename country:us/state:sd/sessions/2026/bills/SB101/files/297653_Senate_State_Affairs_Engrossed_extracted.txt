1 Section 4. That § 12-24-4 be AMENDED:
2 12-24-4. The electors of President and vice president shall, at twelve noon on the

13 Section 5. That a NEW SECTION be added to a NEW CHAPTER in title 12:
14 Each presidential elector nominated pursuant to § 12-5-21 shall execute the
15 following pledge upon nomination: "If selected for the position of presidential elector, I
16 agree to serve and to mark my ballots for the candidates nominated for the offices of
17 president and vice president of the United States, by the party that nominated me to the
18 office of presidential elector." The executed pledges must accompany the submission of
19 the corresponding names to the secretary of state.
20 Each presidential elector of an unaffiliated or independent presidential candidate
21 shall execute the following pledge upon nomination: "If selected for the position of
22 presidential elector for an unaffiliated or independent presidential candidate, I agree to
23 serve and to mark my ballots for that candidate and for that candidate's vice-presidential
24 running mate." The executed pledges must accompany the submission of the
25 corresponding names to the secretary of state.
26 Section 6. That a NEW SECTION be added to a NEW CHAPTER in title 12:
27 At least six days before the day designated by Congress for the election of the

29 ascertainment of appointment. The Governor shall certify the names of the individuals

11 Section 7. That a NEW SECTION be added to a NEW CHAPTER in title 12:
12 The office of presidential elector becomes vacant if the elector:
13 (1) Dies;
14 (2) Resigns;
15 (3) Fails to qualify, as provided by law;
16 (4) Fails to appear at the designated time and place of the meeting;
17 (5) Refuses to vote;
18 (6) Attempts to cast a blank ballot; or
19 (7) Except as provided for in section 8 of this Act, attempts to vote for any candidate
20 other than the candidate whose slate of electors received the highest number of
21 votes in the state's general election.
22 If a presidential elector casts a ballot that results in the office of elector becoming
23 vacant, the ballot may not be accepted or counted. An elector may resign the office of
24 presidential elector by submitting a letter of resignation to the secretary of state.
25 Section 8. That a NEW SECTION be added to a NEW CHAPTER in title 12:
26 If, between the date of the general election and the date designated by Congress
27 for the election of the president and vice president of the United States, the candidate for
28 the president or vice president whose slate of electors received the highest number of
29 votes in the state's general election withdraws, dies, or, because of a permanent disability,
30 becomes unable to discharge the powers and duties of the office to which the candidate
31 is elected, each presidential elector from this state must vote for the replacement
32 candidate nominated according to the bylaws of the party to which the previous candidate
33 belonged.
