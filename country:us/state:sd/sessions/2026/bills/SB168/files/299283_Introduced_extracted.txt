26.252.10 101st Legislative Session 168
2026 South Dakota Legislature
Senate Bill 168
Introduced by: Senator Larson
1 An Act to regulate the use of chatbots by minors.
2 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF SOUTH DAKOTA:
3 Section 1. That a NEW SECTION be added to a NEW CHAPTER in title 26:
4 Terms used in this chapter mean:
5 (1) "Age verification," any method by which a deployer confirms that an individual
6 attempting to engage with an AI-companion, therapy chatbot, or other generative
7 artificial intelligence possessing human-like features is at least eighteen years of
8 age, by authenticating a driver license, passport, or other identification card issued
9 by a state or the federal government, which includes a photograph of the individual
10 and the individual's date of birth;
11 (2) "Artificial intelligence," any machine-based system that, for any explicit or implicit
12 objective, infers from the inputs received by the system how to generate content,
13 decisions, predictions, recommendations, or other outputs, which can influence
14 physical or virtual environments;
15 (3) "AI-generated content," any image, video, audio, multimedia, or text content that
16 is substantially created or modified by generative artificial intelligence, so that the
17 use of generative artificial intelligence alters the meaning or significance a
18 reasonable person would take away from the content;
19 (4) "AI-companion," a generative artificial intelligence that is designed, optimized, and
20 marketed to form ongoing social or emotional bonds with a user, regardless of
21 whether the system also has the ability to provide information, complete tasks, or
22 assist the user with specific functions;
23 (5) "Chatbot," a generative artificial intelligence with which a user is able to interact
24 by approximating or simulating conversation through text, audio, or other
25 communicative medium;
26 (6) "Deployer," an individual or entity that operates or distributes a chatbot;

1 (7) "Emergency situation," a circumstance in which a user indicates to a therapy
2 chatbot or other artificial intelligence that the user is a danger to self or others;
3 (8) "Generative artificial intelligence," an artificial intelligence capable of generating
4 novel image, video, audio, multimedia, or text content based on prompts or other
5 forms of data provided by a user;
6 (9) "Human-like feature," a feature of a generative artificial intelligence capable of:
7 (a) Behaving in a way that would cause a reasonable individual to believe the
8 intelligence that created the image, video, audio, multimedia, or text
9 content is human or possesses sentience, emotions, or desires;
10 (b) Responding to the prompts or data provided by the user in a way that
11 simulates the development of an emotional relationship with the user; or
12 (c) Impersonating any individual, living or dead;
13 (10) "Therapy chatbot," a chatbot that has been designed or modified in a way that
14 purports to provide mental health support, counseling, or therapeutic intervention
15 through the diagnosis, treatment, mitigation, or prevention of a mental health
16 condition; and
17 (11) "User," an individual who interacts with an artificial intelligence;
18 Section 2. That a NEW SECTION be added to a NEW CHAPTER in title 26:
19 A deployer may not make any chatbot operated or distributed by the deployer
20 available to be purchased, used, or otherwise engaged by a minor, if the chatbot is
21 designed, optimized, or marketed as an AI-companion or has been designed or optimized
22 to include any human-like features. The deployer shall implement an age verification
23 system on any chatbot marketed as an AI-companion or designed or optimized to include
24 any human-like features. The deployer may make a version of the chatbot available to a
25 minor, if the deployer has removed all human-like features from the chatbot.
26 Section 3. That a NEW SECTION be added to a NEW CHAPTER in title 26:
27 A deployer shall implement and maintain a system that detects, responds to,
28 reports, and mitigates an emergency situation, in a manner that prioritizes the safety and
29 well-being of the user.

3 Section 5. That a NEW SECTION be added to a NEW CHAPTER in title 26:
4 A deployer may make any chatbot operated or distributed by the deployer available
5 to be purchased, used, or otherwise engaged by a minor, if the chatbot is designed or
6 optimized to be a therapy chatbot. Before a therapy chatbot may be made available to a
7 minor, the deployer must ensure that:
8 (1) The chatbot provides, at the beginning and end of each interaction with the
9 chatbot, a clear and conspicuous disclaimer that the responses are the product of
10 a generative artificial-intelligence and not a licensed professional;
11 (2) The chatbot is not designed or marketed to be a substitute for a licensed
12 professional;
13 (3) A licensed mental health professional:
14 (a) Assesses a user's suitability;
15 (b) Prescribes the chatbot to the user as part of a comprehensive treatment
16 plan; and
17 (c) Monitors the impact of the chatbot on the user;
18 (4) The developer of the chatbot provides robust, independent, and peer-reviewed
19 clinical trial data demonstrating the safety and efficacy of the chatbot, when used
20 under specific conditions; and
21 (5) The system's functions, limitations, and data privacy policies are transparent to
22 both the licensed health professional and the user.
23 Section 6. That a NEW SECTION be added to a NEW CHAPTER in title 26:
24 Any deployer found in violation of sections 2, 3, 4, or 5 of this Act is subject to an
25 injunction and liable for a civil penalty in an amount not exceeding five thousand dollars
26 per incident, per affected minor. The civil penalty may be assessed and recovered only in
27 a civil action by the attorney general. The attorney general shall forward any civil penalty
28 collected under this section to the state treasurer for deposit in the general fund.
29 Before the attorney general may initiate an action pursuant to this section, the
30 attorney general must provide written notice to the deployer. The attorney general shall
31 identify, in the notice, the provision that is alleged to have been violated, and the
32 measures that must be implemented by the deployer to prevent future violations.
