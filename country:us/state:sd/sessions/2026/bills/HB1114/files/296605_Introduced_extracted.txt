26.172.15 101st Legislative Session 1114
2026 South Dakota Legislature
House Bill 1114
Introduced by: Representative Muckey
1 An Act to require that certain mental health information be submitted to and
2 subsequently removed from the National Instant Criminal Background Check
3 System.
4 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF SOUTH DAKOTA:
5 Section 1. That § 23-7-47 be AMENDED:
6 23-7-47. The prosecuting attorney shall report to the attorney general for
7 reporting to the National Instant Criminal Background Check System the name and other
8 identifying information of any person who is acquitted of a crime by reason of insanity
9 pursuant to § 23A-26-5 or who is determined to be incompetent to stand trial pursuant to
10 § 23A-10A-4. The prosecuting attorney shall submit the report to the attorney general, in
11 the manner and form prescribed by the attorney general, within seven working days no
12 later than two hours after the date of the verdict acquitting for insanity or the adjudication
13 of incompetency. The report may not include information relating to the person's diagnosis
14 or treatment.
15 Section 2. That § 23-7-48 be AMENDED:
16 23-7-48. The attorney general shall transmit to the National Instant Criminal
17 Background Check System administered by the Federal Bureau of Investigation the name
18 and other identifying information of any:
19 (1) Any person who is prohibited from possessing a firearm under 18 U.S.C.
20 § 922(g)(4) (January 1, 2026), because the:
21 (a) The person was acquitted of a crime by reason of insanity pursuant to
22 § 23A-26-5, the;
23 (b) The person was determined to be incompetent to stand trial pursuant to
24 § 23A-10A-4, or the; or

7 Section 3. That a NEW SECTION be added to chapter 23-7:
8 The attorney general shall:
9 (1) Transmit any information received in accordance with § 23-7-48 to the National
10 Instant Criminal Background Check System within one working day;
11 (2) Provide a copy of the transmitted information described in subdivision 23-7-48(2)
12 to the individual who is the subject of the transmittal or to the individual's
13 guardian;
14 (3) Request that any of the transmitted information described in subdivision 23-7-
15 48(2) be removed from the National Instant Criminal Background Check System
16 after a period of eighteen months; and
17 (4) Provide to the individual who is the subject of the transmitted information or to the
18 individual's guardian, notice that the information has been removed from the
19 National Instant Criminal Background Check System, as provided for in subdivision
20 (3) of this section.
21 Section 4. That § 27A-10-24 be AMENDED:
22 27A-10-24. If the board of mental illness considers a petition for an involuntary
23 commitment filed in accordance with § 27A-10-1, regardless of whether the board orders
24 or denies an involuntary commitment based on a finding pursuant to § 27A-10-9.1 that
25 the person is a danger to self as defined in subdivision 27A-1-1(7)(a), or a danger to
26 others as defined in subdivision 27A-1-1(6), the chair of the board shall must report to
27 the attorney general for reporting to the National Instant Criminal Background Check
28 System the involuntarily committed person's name and other identifying information of
29 the person who was the subject of the petition. The chair shall submit the report to the
30 attorney general, in the manner and form prescribed by the attorney general, within seven
31 working days after the date of the final order of involuntary commitment within two hours
32 after the determination. The report may not include information relating to the person's
33 diagnosis or treatment.
