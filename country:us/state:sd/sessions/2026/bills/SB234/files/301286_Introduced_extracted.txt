26.990.21 101st Legislative Session 234
2026 South Dakota Legislature
Senate Bill 234
Introduced by: Senator Crabtree
1 An Act to modify provisions pertaining to the purchasing of goods and services
2 related to data center operations.
3 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF SOUTH DAKOTA:
4 Section 1. That a NEW SECTION be added to chapter 10-45:
5 The terms used in this Act mean:
6 (1) "Computer software," programs and other operating information used solely for
7 the maintenance and operation of a qualified data center;
8 (2) "Data center," a centralized repository for the storage, management,
9 dissemination, and processing of electronic data and information, except for that
10 which relates to the mining of digital currency;
11 (3) "Enterprise information technology equipment," the following products used solely
12 for the maintenance and operation of a qualified data center:
13 (a) Computer hardware, servers, storage arrays, backup systems, disaster
14 recovery equipment, routers, cooling systems, temperature control
15 infrastructure, network equipment, switches, load balancers, firewalls,
16 network cabling, edge devices, network monitoring equipment, water
17 treatment systems, chilled water equipment, economizers, cooling towers,
18 and piping and pump equipment for cooling towers;
19 (b) Power infrastructure for the management of electricity, dedicated
20 distribution equipment, backup power generation systems, battery
21 systems, and construction materials for structures unique to data centers
22 or other related infrastructure excluding primary electric generation service,
23 transmission, or utility distribution infrastructure;
24 (c) Racking systems, raised flooring, cabling, or trays necessary for the
25 maintenance and operation of a qualified data center;

1 (d) Security systems, physical access control systems, surveillance systems,
2 biometric scanners, security monitoring equipment, and monitoring
3 systems, environmental monitoring systems, infrastructure management
4 tools, and building management systems;
5 (e) Testing and diagnostic equipment used for maintenance, commissioning, or

7 (f) Telecommunications and fiber infrastructure, fiber optic cabling, satellite
8 equipment, and internet exchange components; and
9 (g) Air quality systems, and specialized HVAC, filtration, and humidity control
10 equipment;
11 (4) "Qualified business," the owner, operator, or tenant of a qualified data center or a
12 proposed data center; and
13 (5) "Qualified data center," a facility located in this state:
14 (a) Comprised of one or more buildings containing a data center;
15 (b) Where all buildings that contain a data center qualify as real property
16 subject to taxation pursuant to §§ 10-4-1 and 10-4-2; and
17 (c) Equipped with fire suppression and prevention systems.
18 Section 2. That a NEW SECTION be added to chapter 10-45:
19 There are specifically exempted from the provisions of this chapter and from the
20 computation of tax imposed by it, gross receipts from the sale of enterprise information
21 technology equipment or computer software to be used in a qualified data center.
22 Section 3. That a NEW SECTION be added to chapter 10-45:
23 To be eligible for the exemption provided in section 2 of this Act, a qualified
24 business in a qualified data center, or a qualified business in a proposed qualified data
25 center, shall submit any documentary evidence required by the Department of Revenue
26 to verify the eligibility of the qualified data center or proposed data center. The
27 documentary evidence required by this section is confidential. The secretary of the
28 Department of Revenue shall determine if the qualified business is eligible.
29 To be eligible for the exemption provided by section 2 of this Act, a qualified
30 business shall:
31 (1) Ensure electric service provided to the qualified business by an electric utility is
32 under a written agreement or rate schedule that avoids the shifting of costs to
33 other customers of the electric utility; and
