26.612.18 101st Legislative Session 1266
2026 South Dakota Legislature
House Bill 1266
Introduced by: Representative Mulally
1 An Act to make certain federal laws applicable to public employees.
2 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF SOUTH DAKOTA:
3 Section 1. That § 2-12-8.2 be AMENDED:
4 2-12-8.2. No elected officer, department or agency head, or division director, or
5 the highest paid employee reporting to such person the officer, head, or director, may be
6 compensated, act, or register as a lobbyist, other than a public employee lobbyist, during
7 a period of two years after that person's termination of service in the state government.
8 A violation of this section is a Class 1 misdemeanor.
9 Section 2. That a NEW SECTION be added to a NEW CHAPTER in title 3:
10 Terms used in this chapter mean:
11 (1) "Political activity," any action directed toward the success or failure of a political
12 party, candidate for partisan public office, candidate for nonpartisan public office,
13 or partisan political group; and
14 (2) "Public employee," any individual holding a position by appointment or employment
15 in the government of this state or in the government of any one or more of the
16 political subdivisions thereof, or in the service of the public schools, or in the service
17 of any authority, commission, or board, or any other branch of the public service.
18 Section 3. That a NEW SECTION be added to a NEW CHAPTER in title 3:
19 A public employee may not:
20 (1) Use the employee's official authority or influence for the purpose of interfering with,
21 or affecting the result of, an election or nomination for office;
22 (2) Knowingly solicit, accept, or receive a contribution, as defined in § 12-27-1, from
23 any person while in the workplace or using state resources;

19 Section 4. That a NEW SECTION be added to a NEW CHAPTER in title 3:
20 A public employee has the right to vote and, subject to the restrictions set forth in
21 section 3 of this Act and to the extent not prohibited by law, a public employee may

23 (1) Expressing opinions on political subjects and candidates;
24 (2) Participating in political management or campaigns;

27 behalf of the candidate, or soliciting votes in support of or in opposition to a
28 candidate;
29 (5) Making contributions of time and money to political parties, committees or other
30 agencies engaged in political action; and
31 (6) Any other political activity, as provided in 5 C.F.R. Part 734 (June 4, 2014).
