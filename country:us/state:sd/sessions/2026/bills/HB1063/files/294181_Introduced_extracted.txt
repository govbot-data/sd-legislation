17 Section 2. That § 36-15-1.1 be AMENDED:
18 36-15-1.1. Natural hair braiding means a form of hair manipulation that results in
19 tension on hair strands by beading, braiding, cornrowing, extending, lacing, locking,
20 twisting, weaving, or wrapping human hair, natural fibers, or synthetic fibers, or hair
21 extensions into a variety of shapes, patterns, and textures, and maintenance thereof, by
22 hand or with clips, combs, hairpins, or thread.
23 Natural hair braiding includes the use of topical agents such as conditioners, gels,
24 moisturizers, oils, pomades, and dry shampoos.
25 Natural hair braiding does not involve the use of penetrating:
26 (1) Penetrating chemical hair treatments, chemical;
27 (2) Chemical hair coloring, straightening, bleaching, or joining agents, chemical hair
28 straightening agents, chemical hair joining agents, permanent;
29 (3) Permanent wave styles, or chemical hair bleaching agents applied to growing
30 human hair;
31 (4) Wet shampoos, conditioners, or other hair treatments; or
32 (5) Clippers, razors, shears, or other tools that alter the natural state of the hair.

11 Section 4. That § 36-15-16.1 be AMENDED:
12 36-15-16.1. Any person engaging solely in natural hair braiding as set forth in
13 § 36-15-1.1 is exempt from the provisions of this chapterA natural hair braider is not
14 required to obtain a license from the commission. A natural hair braider who seeks to
15 provide the practices set forth in § 36-15-1.1 for compensation or any other valuable
16 consideration must:
17 (1) Complete a two-hour course on safety and sanitation procedures, as prescribed by
18 the commission;
19 (2) Apply to the commission for a certificate to perform natural hair braiding,
20 accompanied by a thirty-dollar fee, and display the certificate in the braider's place
21 of business; and
22 (3) Display a sales tax license from the Department of Revenue in the braider's place
23 of business.
24 The commission shall inspect, at least annually, the place of business of a natural
25 hair braider to ensure compliance with the display requirements of this section. If the
26 natural hair braider fails the inspection, the braider may not provide natural hair braiding
27 services for compensation or any other valuable consideration until a reinspection is
28 passed. The commission shall charge the same fee for a reinspection that applies to a
29 salon, as provided in § 36-15-20.
30 For purposes of this section, "natural hair braider" means a person who engages
31 only in the practices set forth in § 36-15-1.1 and not any of the practices of cosmetology.
