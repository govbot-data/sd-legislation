26.549.16 101st Legislative Session 60
2026 South Dakota Legislature
Senate Bill 60
SENATE COMMERCE AND ENERGY ENGROSSED
Introduced by: Senator Crabtree
1 An Act to restrict ownership of real property interests by certain prohibited entities
2 and to provide for enforcement.
3 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF SOUTH DAKOTA:
4 Section 1. That a NEW SECTION be added to a NEW CHAPTER in title 43:
5 Terms used in this chapter mean:
6 (1) "Military installation," any federally or state owned, leased, or operated base,
7 building, camp, post, reservation, or other facility to which active duty or reserve
8 members of the armed forces or civilian employees are assigned pursuant to orders
9 issued by the United States Department of Defense or the South Dakota National
10 Guard;
11 (2) "Prohibited entity," any entity as defined in § 43-2A-1; and
12 (3) "Real property interest," any direct or indirect legal or equitable stake in real
13 property as evidenced by a contract for deed, deed, easement, lease, option,
14 purchase agreement, trust, or other instrument.
15 Section 2. That a NEW SECTION be added to a NEW CHAPTER in title 43:
16 After the expiration of any applicable divestment period provided in section 3 of
17 this Act, a prohibited entity may not, during any time in which there exists a military
18 installation in this state:
19 (1) Hold or obtain any real property interest located, in whole or in part, in this state;
20 or
21 (2) Directly or indirectly control, finance, or hold a legal or equitable interest in any
22 entity that holds a real property interest in this state.
23 Section 3. That a NEW SECTION be added to a NEW CHAPTER in title 43:

1 During any time in which there exists a military installation in this state, a

7 Section 4. That a NEW SECTION be added to a NEW CHAPTER in title 43:
8 The attorney general shall investigate any alleged violation of this chapter.
9 Upon commencing an enforcement action pursuant to section 5 of this Act, the
10 attorney general may, in addition to any authority granted under §§ 15-6-28.2 to 15-6-
11 28.4, inclusive, subpoena from any prohibited entity, any:
12 (1) Contract, purchase agreement, or other transaction document related to a real
13 property interest in this state;
14 (2) Deed or title to the interest;
15 (3) Documentation concerning the financing or ownership of the interest;
16 (4) Documentation identifying any party to a transaction related to the interest; and
17 (5) Any other documentation or information necessary to investigate or substantiate
18 an alleged violation of this chapter.
19 Section 5. That a NEW SECTION be added to a NEW CHAPTER in title 43:
20 The attorney general shall enforce the provisions of this chapter. Any real property
21 interest owned in violation of this chapter must be forfeited to the state or terminated, as
22 applicable.
23 Upon substantiation of an allegation that a prohibited entity holds an interest in
24 this state in violation of this chapter, the attorney general may commence an action, in
25 the circuit court for the county in which the real property is located, for forfeiture or
26 termination of the interest.
27 The court may not order forfeiture or termination of the interest unless the action
28 is brought within three years after evidence of the violation is referred to the attorney
29 general.
30 If the court finds that the interest is held in violation of this chapter, the court
31 must:
32 (1) Enter a judgment declaring the prohibited entity's interest void as of the date of
33 the judgment; and

9 Section 6. That a NEW SECTION be added to a NEW CHAPTER in title 43:
10 Proceeds from the sale of a real property interest ordered pursuant to section 5 of
11 this Act must be distributed in the following order:
12 (1) Payment of the costs of the sale, including all approved fees and expenses, and
13 any taxes and assessments due;
14 (2) Payment, in an amount approved by the court, to the attorney general for
15 reimbursement of investigation and litigation costs and expenses;
16 (3) Payment to bona fide lienholders, in order of priority, except for any lien remaining
17 against the real property under the terms of the sale; and
18 (4) Payment of any remaining proceeds to the person that held the interest
19 immediately before entry of judgment.
20 Section 7. That a NEW SECTION be added to a NEW CHAPTER in title 43:
21 Except as expressly provided in this chapter, a person that is not a prohibited entity
22 does not have any duty to determine or inquire whether another person is a prohibited
23 entity or is otherwise subject to this chapter.
24 A person that is not a prohibited entity may not be held liable under this chapter
25 for failing to determine or inquire whether another person is a prohibited entity.
26 Section 8. That a NEW SECTION be added to a NEW CHAPTER in title 43:
27 Nothing in this chapter:
28 (1) Creates a cause of action against the state or limits the discretion of the attorney
29 general to decline to bring an action under this chapter; or
30 (2) Limits any penalty or remedy set forth in chapter 43-2A regarding the ownership
31 or leasing of agricultural land.
