26.549.15 101st Legislative Session 60
2026 South Dakota Legislature
Senate Bill 60
Introduced by: Senator Crabtree
1 An Act to restrict ownership of certain real property near military installations by
2 certain prohibited entities and to provide for enforcement.
3 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF SOUTH DAKOTA:
4 Section 1. That a NEW SECTION be added to a NEW CHAPTER in title 43:
5 Terms used in this chapter mean:
6 (1) "Military installation," any federally or state owned, leased, or operated base,
7 building, camp, post, reservation, or other facility to which active duty or reserve
8 members of the armed forces or civilian employees are assigned pursuant to orders
9 issued by the United States Department of Defense or the South Dakota National
10 Guard;
11 (2) "Real property interest," any direct or indirect legal or equitable stake in real
12 property as evidenced by a contract for deed, deed, easement, last will and
13 testament, lease, option, purchase agreement, trust, or other instrument; and
14 (3) "Restricted zone," an area within a ten-mile radius, measured in a straight line,
15 from any point on the boundary of a military installation.
16 Section 2. That a NEW SECTION be added to a NEW CHAPTER in title 43:
17 After the expiration of any applicable divestment period provided in section 3 of
18 this Act, a prohibited entity, as defined in § 43-2A-1, may not:
19 (1) Hold or obtain any real property interest located, in whole or in part, within a
20 restricted zone; or
21 (2) Directly or indirectly control, finance, or hold a legal or equitable interest in any
22 entity that holds a real property interest in a restricted zone.
23 Section 3. That a NEW SECTION be added to a NEW CHAPTER in title 43:

1 A prohibited entity, as defined in § 43-2A-1, which acquires a real property interest
2 in a restricted zone:
3 (1) Through descent or devise, must dispose of the interest within three years of

7 Section 4. That a NEW SECTION be added to a NEW CHAPTER in title 43:
8 For any transfer of a real property interest in a restricted zone, a transferee shall
9 execute an affidavit, under penalty of perjury, declaring that, as of the date of the transfer,
10 the transferee's acquisition of the interest does not violate this chapter. The affidavit must
11 be recorded in the office of the register of deeds of the county in which the interest is
12 located.
13 The transferee's failure to execute an affidavit under this section does not:
14 (1) Affect the marketability or insurability of the title to the interest; or
15 (2) Subject a closing agent, real estate broker or salesperson, or title insurance
16 company to civil or criminal liability, unless the person has actual knowledge that
17 the transaction is in violation of this chapter.
18 The electronic recording commission shall, by rule promulgated pursuant to
19 chapter 1-26, establish a standard form for the affidavit required by this section.
20 Section 5. That a NEW SECTION be added to a NEW CHAPTER in title 43:
21 The attorney general shall investigate any alleged violation of this chapter.
22 Upon commencing an enforcement action pursuant to section 6 of this Act, the
23 attorney general may, in addition to any authority granted under §§ 15-6-28.2 to 15-6-
24 28.4, inclusive, subpoena from any foreign entity, foreign government, foreign person,
25 prohibited entity, or resident, as those terms are defined in § 43-2A-1, any:
26 (1) Contract, purchase agreement, or other transaction document related to a real
27 property interest in a restricted zone;
28 (2) Deed or title to the interest;
29 (3) Documentation concerning the financing or ownership of the interest;
30 (4) Documentation identifying any party to a transaction related to the interest; and
31 (5) Any other documentation or information necessary to investigate or substantiate
32 an alleged violation of this chapter.

1 Section 6. That a NEW SECTION be added to a NEW CHAPTER in title 43:
2 The attorney general shall enforce the provisions of this chapter. Any real property
3 interest owned in violation of this chapter must be forfeited to the state or terminated, as
4 applicable.
5 Upon substantiation of an allegation that a prohibited entity holds an interest in a
6 restricted zone in violation of this chapter, the attorney general shall commence an action,
7 in the circuit court for the county in which the real property is located, for forfeiture or
8 termination of the interest.
9 The court may not order forfeiture or termination of the interest unless the action
10 is brought within three years after evidence of the violation is referred to the attorney
11 general.
12 If the court finds that the interest is held in violation of this chapter, the court
13 must:
14 (1) Enter a judgment declaring the prohibited entity's interest void as of the date of
15 the judgment; and
16 (2) Order that the interest be forfeited or terminated, as applicable;
17 If the court orders forfeiture of the interest, the court must enter a judgment
18 directing the attorney general to sell the property and specifying that the sale is for the
19 benefit of the person that held the interest immediately before entry of the judgment,
20 subject to the distribution of proceeds pursuant to section 7 of this Act.
21 Upon commencing an action pursuant to this section, the attorney general shall
22 cause a notice of lis pendens to be recorded with the register of deeds for the county in
23 which the interest is located.
24 Section 7. That a NEW SECTION be added to a NEW CHAPTER in title 43:
25 Proceeds from the sale of a real property interest ordered pursuant to section 6 of
26 this Act must be distributed in the following order:
27 (1) Payment of the costs of the sale, including all approved fees and expenses, and
28 any taxes and assessments due;
29 (2) Payment, in an amount approved by the court, to the attorney general for
30 reimbursement of investigation and litigation costs and expenses;
31 (3) Payment to bona fide lienholders, in order of priority, except for any lien remaining
32 against the real property under the terms of the sale; and

14 Section 10. That a NEW SECTION be added to a NEW CHAPTER in title 43:
15 This chapter does not apply to:
16 (1) Any real property interest held by a publicly traded company in the United States,
17 which was acquired before July 1, 2026;
18 (2) The exchange of a real property interest approved by the governing body of a
19 county, municipality, or other political subdivision of this state; or
20 (3) Any real property interest held by a prohibited entity solely for the purpose of
21 securing indebtedness, if the prohibited entity does not take possession of, or
22 obtain voting or managerial control over, the real property interest.
