26.343.19 101st Legislative Session 1212
2026 South Dakota Legislature
House Bill 1212
Introduced by: Representative Randolph
1 An Act to revise provisions in order to strengthen protections for unborn children.
2 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF SOUTH DAKOTA:
3 Section 1. That § 21-5-1 be AMENDED:
4 21-5-1. Whenever If the death or injury of a person of an individual, including an
5 unborn child, shall be is caused by a wrongful act, neglect, or default, and the act, neglect,
6 or default is such as would have entitled the party injured to maintain that would otherwise
7 entitle the individual to bring an action and recover damages in respect thereto, if death
8 had not ensued, then and in every such case, the corporation which, or the person who,
9 would have been liable, if death had not ensued, had the individual survived, the
10 wrongdoer, or the personal representative of the estate of such person as such personal
11 representative, shall be the wrongdoer, is liable, to an action for damages,
12 notwithstanding the death of the person injured, and although the death shall have been
13 caused under such circumstances as amount in law to a felony; and when the action is
14 against such personal representative, the damages recovered shall be a valid claim against
15 the estate of such deceased person. However, an action under this section involving an
16 unborn child shall be for the exclusive benefit of the mother or the lawfully married parents
17 of the unborn child.
18 Section 2. That a NEW SECTION be added to chapter 22-16:
19 The provisions of this chapter apply equally to an offense committed against an
20 unborn child. The provisions of this chapter do not apply to the unintentional death of an
21 unborn child resulting from:
22 (1) Lifesaving procedures undertaken to preserve the life of a pregnant mother, if
23 accompanied by reasonable and available steps to preserve the life of the mother's
24 unborn child; or
25 (2) A spontaneous miscarriage.

5 Section 3. That § 22-16-1.1 be REPEALED.
6 Homicide is fetal homicide if the person knew, or reasonably should have known,
7 that a woman bearing an unborn child was pregnant and caused the death of the unborn
8 child without lawful justification and if the person:
9 (1) Intended to cause the death of or do serious bodily injury to the pregnant woman
10 or the unborn child; or
11 (2) Knew that the acts taken would cause death or serious bodily injury to the pregnant
12 woman or her unborn child; or
13 (3) If perpetrated without any design to effect death by a person engaged in the
14 commission of any felony.
15 Fetal homicide is a Class B felony.
16 This section does not apply to acts which cause the death of an unborn child if
17 those acts were committed during any abortion, lawful or unlawful, to which the pregnant
18 woman consented.
19 Section 4. That § 22-17-5.2 be REPEALED.
20 A female who undergoes an unlawful abortion, as set forth in § 22-17-5.1, may
21 not be held criminally liable for the abortion.
