1 An Act to clarify documentation requirements for assistance animals in rental
2 dwelling units.
3 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF SOUTH DAKOTA:
4 Section 1. That § 43-32-33 be AMENDED:
5 43-32-33. For the purposes of Terms used in §§ 43-32-34 to 43-32-36, inclusive,
6 the term, service animal, refers to, mean:
7 (1) "Assistance animal," any animal that serves a role for an individual with a disability
8 as an emotional support animal, any, service, or therapy animal, or any assistance
9 animal, and the term, disability, is in a manner that is necessary for the health of
10 an individual; and
11 (2) "Disability," a physical or mental impairment that substantially limits one or more
12 major life activities of a person an individual.
13 Section 2. That § 43-32-34 be AMENDED:
14 43-32-34. A Except as otherwise provided in this section, a landlord may require
15 a tenant of the landlord's rental dwelling unit to provide reliable supporting documentation
16 be provided by a tenant of a rental dwelling unit, if the tenant asserts a disability requiring,
17 under any provision of law, that a service animal or an assistance animal be allowed as
18 an accommodation on the rented premises.
19 A The landlord may not require supporting documentation from a the tenant if the
20 tenant's disability or disability-related need for a service animal or an assistance animal
21 is readily apparent or already known to the landlord.
22 The landlord is not required to grant a reasonable accommodation for an assistance
23 animal if:
24 (1) The rental dwelling unit is in an owner-occupied building with four or fewer units;

9 Section 3. That § 43-32-35 be AMENDED:
10 43-32-35. The Reliable supporting documentation shall provided by a tenant
11 pursuant to § 43-32-34:
12 (1) Must confirm the tenant's disability and the relationship between the tenant's
13 disability and the need for the requested accommodation. The documentation
14 shall;
15 (2) Must be issued no more than one year before the date of the request for
16 accommodation, unless the disability or disability-related need is readily apparent,
17 permanent, or ongoing;
18 (3) Must originate from a licensed health care provider who does not operate in this
19 state solely to provide certification for service or assistance animals:
20 (a) Is acting within the scope of the provider's licensure;
21 (b) Has personal knowledge of the tenant through a professional provider-
22 patient relationship;
23 (c) Conducted an individualized assessment of the tenant; and
24 (d) Is familiar with the tenant's disability and functional limitations;
25 (4) Must be on the provider's official letterhead, containing the provider's contact
26 information and medical license number; and
27 (5) May be obtained through online or telehealth services, provided that the
28 documentation is issued only after final review and a written attestation by the
29 provider that the documentation reflects an individualized determination of the
30 tenant's disability-related need for the requested accommodation.
31 A landlord may verify the authenticity of supporting documentation by confirming
32 the provider's licensure and that the documentation was issued specifically for the tenant.
