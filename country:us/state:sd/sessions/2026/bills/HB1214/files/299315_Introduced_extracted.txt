26.412.11 101st Legislative Session 1214
2026 South Dakota Legislature
House Bill 1214
Introduced by: Representative Wittman
1 An Act to require that courts consider as a mitigating factor during sentencing an
2 individual's history as a victim of abuse and provide for a reduced sentence
3 in certain circumstances.
4 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF SOUTH DAKOTA:
5 Section 1. That a NEW SECTION be added to chapter 23A-27:
6 Terms used in sections 1 to 12, inclusive, of this Act, mean:
7 (1) "Abuse," physical, psychological, or sexual abuse threatened by, or actually
8 inflicted upon an individual by, a current or former family member, household
9 member, romantic or sexual partner, spouse, or trafficker of the individual, or by
10 another who used the individual for financial gain;
11 (2) "Physical abuse," any physical injury or damage to the body that is not accidental;
12 (3) "Psychological abuse," a pattern of coercive control, economic or financial control,
13 humiliation, mental intimidation, or threats, that provokes fear of harm; and
14 (4) "Sexual abuse," any sexual contact or behavior that is perpetrated without consent,
15 by force, or under unequal or coercive conditions.
16 Section 2. That a NEW SECTION be added to chapter 23A-27:
17 Before an individual's sentence is imposed, the court shall consider as a mitigating
18 factor, if applicable, the individual's history as a victim of abuse.
19 The individual shall provide to the court documentary evidence corroborating that
20 the individual was, at the time of the offense, a victim of abuse, with at least one piece of
21 evidence being a:
22 (1) Court record;
23 (2) Presentence report;
24 (3) Social services record;
25 (4) Hospital record;

1 (5) Sworn statement from a witness to the abuse who is not the individual;
2 (6) Law enforcement record; or
3 (7) Protective order.
4 Section 3. That a NEW SECTION be added to chapter 23A-27:
5 In addition to the evidence required by section 2 of this Act, the individual may
6 provide to the court:
7 (1) Jail records;
8 (2) Department of Corrections records;
9 (3) Documentation prepared at or near the time of the commission or prosecution of
10 an offense tending to support the individual's claim of abuse;
11 (4) Verification of consultation with:
12 (a) A licensed health care provider;
13 (b) A licensed mental health care provider;
14 (c) An employee of a court acting within the scope of employment;
15 (d) A member of the clergy;
16 (e) An attorney; or
17 (f) A social worker, rape crisis counselor, or other advocate acting on behalf of
18 an agency that assists victims of abuse; and
19 (5) Expert testimony from a psychiatrist, psychologist, or mental health professional
20 showing that the individual was diagnosed with post-traumatic stress disorder as
21 a result of the abuse.
22 Section 4. That a NEW SECTION be added to chapter 23A-27:
23 If the court finds by clear and convincing evidence that, at the time of the offense,
24 the individual was a victim of abuse, and the abuse was related to and was a substantial
25 contributing factor to the crime for which sentence is being rendered, the court must
26 reduce the penalty provided by law for the offense as follows:
27 (1) A sentence of life imprisonment in a state correctional facility without the possibility
28 of parole must be reduced to thirty-five years or less;
29 (2) A sentence of life imprisonment in a state correctional facility with the possibility
30 of parole must be reduced to thirty years or less; and
31 (3) A sentence to a term of years in a state correctional facility must be reduced by at
32 least one-third.

1 Section 5. That a NEW SECTION be added to chapter 23A-27:
2 If the offense is a crime of violence, as defined in § 22-1-2, in order to receive the
3 sentence reduction provided in section 4 of this Act, the individual must prove by clear
4 and convincing evidence that the victim of the individual was the perpetrator of the
5 individual's abuse.
6 Section 6. That a NEW SECTION be added to chapter 23A-27:
7 The provisions of sections 1 to 12, inclusive, of this Act do not apply to an individual
8 convicted of:
9 (1) A Class A felony;
10 (2) An offense that would require the individual to register as a sex offender under
11 chapter 22-24B; or
12 (2) An attempt or conspiracy to commit an offense that would require the individual to
13 register as a sex offender.
14 Section 7. That a NEW SECTION be added to chapter 23A-27:
15 An individual serving a sentence for an offense committed prior to July 1, 2026,
16 may submit, to the court that imposed the individual's original sentence, a request to
17 apply for resentencing. The request must include documentation showing the individual
18 is:
19 (1) Confined in a state correctional facility under the custody and control of the
20 Department of Corrections;
21 (2) Serving a sentence for an offense committed prior to July 1, 2026; and
22 (3) Eligible for an alternative sentence as a victim of abuse.
23 If the court finds the requirements are met, the court must notify the individual
24 that the individual may apply for resentencing. Upon notification, the individual may
25 request that the court appoint an attorney to assist in the preparation of and proceedings
26 on the application for resentencing.
27 If the court finds the requirements are not met, the court must notify the individual
28 and deny the individual's request without prejudice.
29 Section 8. That a NEW SECTION be added to chapter 23A-27:

2 individual's original sentence, the same evidence required by section 2 of this Act. The

6 If the application does comply with the requirements of section 2 of this Act, the

10 Upon receipt of an application for resentencing, the clerk of court shall notify and
11 provide a copy of the application to the appropriate state's attorney.
12 Section 9. That a NEW SECTION be added to chapter 23A-27:
13 At a resentencing hearing pursuant to section 8 of this Act, the court shall
14 determine any controverted issues of fact relevant to the issue of sentencing. The court
15 may consider any facts or circumstances relevant to the imposition of a new sentence
16 submitted by the individual or the state's attorney.
17 The court may consider the individual's institutional record of confinement, but the
18 institutional record may not be solely dispositive as to whether the individual receives a
19 new sentence. Consideration of the institutional record may include the individual's
20 participation in programming and treatment while incarcerated and the individual's
21 disciplinary history. The inability of the individual to participate in programming or
22 treatment despite the individual's willingness to do so may not be considered an
23 aggravating factor.
24 The court may not order a new presentence investigation or entertain any matter
25 challenging the underlying basis of the individual's original conviction.

29 notify the individual and enter an order that includes written findings of fact and the
30 reasons for the court's determination. If the individual is denied resentencing on the merits
31 of the individual's application, the court must deny the application with prejudice.
32 If the court determines that the individual may be resentenced in accordance with
