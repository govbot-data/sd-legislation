26.747.9 101st Legislative Session 1126
2026 South Dakota Legislature
House Bill 1126
Introduced by: Representative Walburg
1 An Act to revise a provision related to driving under the influence.
2 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF SOUTH DAKOTA:
3 Section 1. That § 32-23-4.9 be AMENDED:
4 32-23-4.9. If a conviction for a violation of § 32-23-1 is for a sixth or subsequent
5 offense, and the person had at least five convictions of § 32-23-1 occurring within twenty-
6 five years of the violation being charged, and at least two of those prior convictions
7 occurred within ten years, the violation is an aggravated offense and the person is guilty
8 of a Class 4 felony. If a person is convicted of an aggravated violation of § 32-23-1 offense
9 and the person has at least six five convictions of § 32-23-1 occurring within fifteen years
10 of the violation being charged, the court must sentence the person to at least six years in
11 a state correctional facility, one year of which must be served on parole, unless refused
12 pursuant to § 24-15A-15. Any term of parole must include at least one of the following:
13 enrollment in an alcohol or drug accountability program, an ignition interlock, a breath
14 alcohol interlock, an alcohol monitoring bracelet, or another enhanced monitoring tool.
15 The court may suspend this sentence only if the court orders the person to participate in
16 and complete a drug court program, DUI court program, veterans treatment court
17 program, or mental health court program, as a condition of probation.
18 The court must revoke the person's driver license for a period of not less than three
19 years from the date the sentence is imposed, or three years from the date of initial release
20 from imprisonment, whichever is later. If the person is returned to imprisonment prior to
21 the completion of the period of driver license revocation, time spent imprisoned does not
22 count toward fulfilling the period of revocation. If the person is convicted of driving without
23 a license during that period, the court must sentence the person to the county jail for not
24 less than twenty days, which sentence may not be suspended. Notwithstanding § 23A-
25 27-19, the court retains jurisdiction to modify the conditions of the license revocation for
26 the term of the revocation.
