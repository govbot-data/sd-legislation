1 An Act to require that social media companies provide collected personal data of a
2 user at the user's request and maintain transparent interoperability
3 interfaces.
4 BE IT ENACTED BY THE LEGISLATURE OF THE STATE OF SOUTH DAKOTA:

10 Section 3. That a NEW SECTION be added to chapter 53-12:
11 If a user requests a copy of the user's personal data being held by a social media
12 service with more than five hundred thousand active monthly users and whose primary
13 focus is not charity or religion, the social media service must provide the personal data in
14 a format that:
15 (1) Is portable to the extent technically feasible;
16 (2) Is readily usable to the extent practicable; and
17 (3) Allows the user to transmit the data to another social media service, without
18 impediment.
19 Section 4. That a NEW SECTION be added to chapter 53-12:
20 A social media company operating a social media service with more than five
21 hundred thousand active monthly users and whose primary focus is not charity or religion
22 shall implement a transparent, third-party-accessible interoperability interface subject to
23 section 3 of this Act to allow the social media service's users to choose to:
24 (1) Share a common set of the user's personal data between the social media services
25 designated by the user; and
26 (2) Enable third parties to access content created by the user and to be notified when
27 new or updated content is available, with the user's permission.
28 Section 5. That a NEW SECTION be added to chapter 53-12:
29 To achieve interoperability under section 4 of this Act, a social media company
30 operating a social media service with more than five hundred thousand active monthly
31 users and whose primary focus is not charity or religion shall:

1 (1) Utilize an open protocol;
2 (2) Facilitate and maintain interoperability and synchronous data sharing with other
3 social media services based on terms that do not discriminate between social media
4 services;
5 (3) Establish proportionate thresholds related to the frequency, nature, and volume of
6 requests, beyond which the social media company may assess a fee for the access;
7 (4) Offer to other social media companies a functionally equivalent version of any
8 internal interfaces created by the social media company for the social media
9 company's own social media services;
10 (5) Disclose to other social media companies complete, accurate, and regularly
11 updated documentation describing access to the interoperability interface required
12 under this section;
13 (6) Refrain from sharing or receiving a user's personal data through the interoperability
14 interface except with the user's consent; and
15 (7) Adopt a method for users to give consent for data sharing with other social media
16 services or third parties through the interoperability interface.
17 Section 6. That a NEW SECTION be added to chapter 53-12:
18 A social media company operating a social media service with more than five
19 hundred thousand active monthly users and whose primary focus is not charity or religion
20 shall secure all personal data obtained through an interoperability interface and safeguard
21 the privacy and security of a user's personal data obtained from other social media
22 services through the interoperability interface, in accordance with the social media
23 company's privacy notice and administrative, technical, and physical data security
24 practices.
25 Section 7. That a NEW SECTION be added to chapter 53-12:
26 Sections 3 to 6, inclusive, of this Act do not apply to an entity that is owned,
27 controlled, operated, or maintained by a religious organization and is exempt from
28 property taxation under state law.
