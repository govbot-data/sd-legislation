7 Section 3. That a NEW SECTION be added to chapter 53-12:
8 If a user requests a copy of the user's personal data being held by a social media
9 service with more than one hundred million active monthly users and whose primary focus
10 is not charity or religion, the social media service must provide the personal data in a
11 format that:
12 (1) Is portable to the extent technically feasible;
13 (2) Is readily usable to the extent practicable; and
14 (3) Allows the user to transmit the data to another social media service, without
15 impediment.
16 Section 4. That a NEW SECTION be added to chapter 53-12:
17 A social media company operating a social media service with more than one
18 hundred million active monthly users and whose primary focus is not charity or religion
19 shall implement a transparent, third-party-accessible interoperability interface subject to
20 section 3 of this Act to allow the social media service's users to choose to:
21 (1) Expose a common set of the user's personal data to other social media services;
22 and
23 (2) Enable third parties to access content created by the user and to be notified when
24 new or updated content is available, with the user's permission.
25 Section 5. That a NEW SECTION be added to chapter 53-12:
26 To achieve interoperability under section 4 of this Act, a social media company
27 operating a social media service with more than one hundred million active monthly users
28 and whose primary focus is not charity or religion shall provide users with the ability to
29 export their social graph to social media services in the following manner:

1 (1) The social media service shall allow a user the ability to choose whether to export
2 all of a user's social graph data or export only a subset of that data, and a user
3 may select a subset of the data by selecting:
4 (a) Certain types of data;
5 (b) Certain dates of data; or
6 (c) Data generated since a previous transfer;
7 (2) A social media service shall make the export available in a machine readable
8 format;
9 (3) A social media service shall make the export using a publicly available technical
10 standard that is free from:
11 (a) Licensing fees; and
12 (b) Patent restrictions that any social media service can freely use;
13 (4) The social media service shall allow a user to choose between a single export or
14 continuous, ongoing exports, which must occur at least every twenty-four hours;
15 (5) The social media service shall ensure that the exports are done in a manner
16 consistent with industry best practices for privacy and security; and
17 (6) The social media service providing the export may impose reasonable terms on the
18 transfer, including terms to ensure privacy and security, provided that the terms
19 do not discriminate between social media services and do not unfairly preference
20 the social media service's own service over others.
21 Nothing in this section restricts a social media platform's ability to detect, prevent,
22 protect against, or respond to a security incident, risk to safety or integrity, harassment,
23 malicious or fraudulent activity, or any illegal activity. Nothing in this section restricts a
24 social media platform's ability to comply with any legal obligations.

26 A social media company operating a social media service with more than one

30 services through the interoperability interface, in accordance with the social media
31 company's privacy notice and administrative, technical, and physical data security
32 practices.
