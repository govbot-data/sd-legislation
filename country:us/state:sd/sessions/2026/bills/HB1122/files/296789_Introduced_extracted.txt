22 Section 2. That a NEW SECTION be added to chapter 41-6:
23 Except as otherwise provided in this section, beginning July 1, 2027, an individual
24 may not, for consideration, act as a fishing guide or represent to the public that the
25 individual is a fishing guide, without first obtaining an annual guide license from the
26 Department of Game, Fish and Parks. The licensure requirement does not apply to an
27 individual providing guide services on a body of water located entirely within the
28 boundaries of property that the individual owns.
29 Any individual who provides fishing guide services without a license, or while under
30 a suspended license, is guilty of a Class 1 misdemeanor. Each client who receives service
31 under these conditions constitutes a distinct and separate offense.

1 An individual seeking licensure as a fishing guide must submit an application to the
2 Department of Game, Fish and Parks. The department shall make the application available
3 on its website and in printed form upon request.
4 To be eligible for licensure as a fishing guide, an individual must:
5 (1) Be at least eighteen years of age;
6 (2) Maintain, in an amount of one million dollars per occurrence, general liability
7 insurance against claims of damage or injury resulting from the guiding activity;
8 and
9 (3) Pay the licensure fee established under section 5 of this Act.
10 Section 4. That a NEW SECTION be added to chapter 41-6:
11 The Department of Game, Fish and Parks may, in accordance with chapter 1-26,
12 deny an application for licensure as a fishing guide, or suspend or revoke a guide license,
13 upon a determination that:
14 (1) The applicant or licensee materially misrepresented any information required on
15 the application for a license;
16 (2) During the five-year period preceding the date of the application, the applicant or
17 licensee knowingly provided guide services to an individual who was not
18 appropriately licensed to engage in the activity, as required in chapter 41-6;
19 (3) During the five-year period preceding the date of the application, the applicant or
20 licensee, while acting as a guide, engaged in conduct detrimental to the image and
21 integrity of the guide industry;
22 (4) During the five-year period preceding the date of the application, the applicant or
23 licensee was convicted of an offense that has a direct bearing on the ability to serve
24 clients as a guide;
25 (5) During the five-year period preceding the date of the application, the applicant or
26 licensee violated any statute or rule related to fishing or guiding, in any jurisdiction;
27 (6) During the five-year period preceding the date of the application, the applicant or
28 licensee had a guide license denied, suspended, or revoked in any jurisdiction; or
29 (7) The applicant or licensee pled guilty to, nolo contendere to, or was convicted of, a
30 felony.
31 Section 5. That a NEW SECTION be added to chapter 41-6:
32 The Department of Game, Fish and Parks shall charge a fee of:
33 (1) One hundred fifty dollars for a resident fishing guide license; and
